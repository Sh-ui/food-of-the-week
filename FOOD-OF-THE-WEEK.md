## Weekend Prep

##### BatchDay
###### saturday: thaw salmon + chicken + beef / sunday: chicken bag + broccoli bag + shape meatballs

### Prep Checklist

**Saturday afternoon:**

- [ ] Move **salmon** from deep freezer to fridge to thaw overnight (for Monday).
- [ ] Move **chicken thighs** (both packs) from deep freezer to fridge to thaw overnight (for Thursday SV).
- [ ] Move **ground beef** from deep freezer to fridge to thaw overnight (for Tuesday meatballs).

**Sunday:**

- [ ] **Chicken thigh bag** -- season thawed thighs with **salt**, **black pepper**, and 2 smashed **garlic cloves**. Vac seal flat. SV 160°F / 2 h. Ice bath, refrigerate. These are Thursday's orange chicken.
- [ ] After chicken is done, raise bath to **183°F**. **Broccoli bag** -- cut *1 large head* **broccoli** into florets, season with a pinch of **salt** and a drizzle of **sesame oil**, vac seal. SV 183°F / 8 min. Ice bath, refrigerate. This is Thursday's sesame cai.
- [ ] **Feta meatballs** (for Tuesday): combine the thawed **ground beef** in a bowl with *1/2 cup* crumbled **feta**, *1 tsp* **dried oregano**, *1/2 tsp* **cumin**, *1/2 tsp* **black pepper**, *1/2 tsp* **salt**, and *1 minced garlic clove*. Mix gently -- don't overwork. Roll into golf-ball-sized meatballs. Arrange on a plate, cover, refrigerate. Makes about 16-18 meatballs.

### Workflow

Saturday is bag moves only. Sunday: drop the **chicken bag** in the bath first (160°F / 2 h). While it runs, shape the **meatballs** and refrigerate. Once chicken is done -- ice bath, bag in the fridge -- raise the bath to 183°F and run the **broccoli bag** (8 min). Done by early afternoon.

### Protein Bags

**Orange chicken thighs** (Thursday): season with salt, black pepper, 2 smashed garlic cloves. Vac seal flat. SV 160°F / 2 h. Ice bath, refrigerate.

## Sheet Pan Miso Salmon

##### Prep&Heat
###### prep + miso glaze + kimchi salad + 5:30 rice + sheet pan in

#### Anchor:
**Salmon** fillets glazed with miso, roasted at high heat on one sheet pan with scattered **frozen edamame** and **onion wedges**. The edamame crisps at the edges; the onion sweetens.

#### Fan:
White rice

#### Cai:
Cucumber kimchi salad -- sliced cucumber, kimchi, scallion, a drop of sesame oil. Cold and sharp.

Dressed greens -- bagged salad mix, soy-ginger dressing from the fridge.

### Already Prepped

- **Salmon** thawed overnight in the fridge from Saturday.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Line a large **sheet pan** with foil. Preheat the **oven to 425°F**.
- [ ] **Miso glaze**: whisk together in a small bowl: *2 tbsp* **white miso**, *1 tbsp* **sesame oil**, *1 tbsp* **soy sauce**, *1 tsp* **sugar**, *1 tbsp* **cooking wine** (mirin or sake). Set near the oven.
- [ ] Cut *1* **yellow onion** into thick wedges (6-8 wedges). Toss with a drizzle of **oil** and pinch of **salt**.
- [ ] Measure out *2 cups* **frozen shelled edamame** -- no need to thaw, they go on the pan frozen.
- [ ] Pat the **salmon** fillets dry with paper towel.
- [ ] At **5:30** start **white rice** in the rice cooker.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Cucumber kimchi salad**: slice *1 large* **cucumber** into thin half-moons. Into a bowl with a generous scoop of **kimchi** (roughly equal volume to the cucumber), *2 scallion greens* sliced thin, a few drops of **sesame oil**. Toss. Taste -- it should be tart, a little spicy, crunchy. Refrigerate.

Arrange **onion wedges** and **edamame** on the sheet pan. Nestle **salmon** fillets among them skin-side down. Brush the **miso glaze** generously over the salmon tops and lightly over the edamame and onion.

### Chef - Cooking

Slide the **sheet pan** into the **425°F oven**. Roast for **12-15 min** until the salmon flakes easily at the thickest point and the glaze has caramelized to a deep amber at the edges. The edamame should have a few charred spots. No flipping needed.

Transfer salmon to a serving platter. Use a spatula to pile the **edamame and onion** around it. Drizzle any pan juices over the top.

Just before serving, toss the **bagged greens** in a large bowl with a few spoonfuls of **soy-ginger dressing**. Taste.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Salmon platter** in the center. **Cucumber kimchi salad** and **dressed greens** in shared dishes.

## Mediterranean Orzo with Feta Meatballs

##### WokNight
###### prep + chickpea salad + orzo water + wok sequence

#### Anchor:
**Ground beef and feta meatballs** -- seared in the wok until deeply crusted, herby and savory inside, plated over warm lemon-parsley orzo.

#### Fan:
Lemon-parsley orzo -- tossed with olive oil, lemon zest and juice, fresh parsley, garlic.

#### Cai:
Chickpea + tomato + cucumber + onion salad -- lemon juice, olive oil, parsley, cold.

Wok-fired green beans -- cumin, coriander, black pepper, garlic. Dry and charred.

### Already Prepped

- **Feta meatballs** shaped and refrigerated from Sunday.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Drain and rinse *1 can* **chickpeas**.
- [ ] Finely dice *1* **roma or plum tomato** into small cubes.
- [ ] Dice *1/2* **cucumber** into small cubes.
- [ ] Finely dice *1/4* **yellow onion**, rinse under cold water 10 seconds, drain.
- [ ] Chop a generous handful of **parsley** -- half for the chickpea salad, half for the orzo.
- [ ] Zest and halve *2* **lemons**.
- [ ] Trim the ends off the **green beans**.
- [ ] Fill a large pot with water and **salt it generously**. Bring to a boil for the orzo.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Chickpea salad**: combine the **chickpeas**, diced **tomato**, diced **cucumber**, **rinsed diced onion**, *half the parsley*, juice of *1 lemon*, *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, a generous pinch of **salt** and **black pepper**. Toss. Taste -- bright, lemony, savory. Refrigerate.

**Orzo**: cook in the boiling salted water until just al dente (follow pkg time minus 1 min -- it will continue cooking as you dress it). Drain, immediately return to the pot off heat. Toss with *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, *lemon zest* of 1 lemon, juice of *half a lemon*, *the remaining parsley*, *1 minced or pressed garlic clove*, generous **salt** and **black pepper**. Pour into a wide serving bowl. It should be glossy and fragrant. Hold warm.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

**Wok dish 1 -- Meatballs**: Wok ripping hot, generous **oil** -- enough to coat the bottom. Place the **meatballs** in without crowding (work in batches if needed). *Don't move* for 90 seconds until the bottom is deeply browned. Roll to the next face, repeat. Work all 3-4 sides until a dark crust is set all around, 8-10 min total. They'll be cooked through by the time they're fully crusted. Transfer to a plate. ***Taste one*** -- adjust salt if needed.

**Wok dish 2 -- Green beans**: quick rinse of the wok. Back to ripping hot, splash of **oil**. Dump the **green beans** in, spread flat, *don't touch* 60-90 seconds until blistered in spots. Toss. Add *2 cloves* **minced garlic**, toss 20 seconds. Add *1 tsp* **cumin** + *1/2 tsp* **coriander** + *generous* **black pepper** + pinch of **salt**. Toss hard until coated and fragrant. Off heat.

**Table**: spoon **orzo** into a wide center bowl. Arrange **meatballs** on top. **Chickpea salad** and **green beans** in shared dishes. Extra **lemon wedges** on the side.

## Orange Chicken

##### WokNight
###### prep + carrot salad + soup pot on + 5:30 rice + wok sequence

#### Anchor:
Cornstarch-crusted **chicken thigh** (from Sunday SV bag) fried in the wok for color, tossed in an orange-soy glaze with **bell pepper** strips cooked in.

#### Fan:
White rice

#### Cai:
Ribbon carrot salad -- vegetable peeler ribbons, scallion greens, soy-ginger dressing, cold.

Sesame broccoli -- **broccoli** from Sunday SV bag wok-charred, garlic, sesame oil and seeds finish.

Clear soup -- light broth with fresh ginger and scallion, ladled at the table.

### Already Prepped

- **Chicken thigh bag** in fridge from Sunday SV (160°F / 2 h, fully cooked).
- **Broccoli bag** in fridge from Sunday SV (183°F / 8 min, fully cooked).

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Pull the **chicken bag** and **broccoli bag** from the fridge -- set on the counter 30 min before cook time.
- [ ] Open the **chicken bag**, cut thighs into large chunks (3-4 pieces per thigh). Into a bowl. Set near the stove.
- [ ] Use a vegetable peeler to make **carrot ribbons**: peel along the length of *3-4 large* **carrots**, rotating as you go. Stop at the seedy core. Into a bowl. Refrigerate.
- [ ] Slice *2* **bell peppers** into strips. Set near the stove.
- [ ] Slice *4-5* **scallions** -- whites reserved for the wok, greens for garnish and carrot salad.
- [ ] **Orange sauce**: in a small bowl whisk together: juice of *3* **oranges**, zest of *1 orange*, *4 tbsp* **soy sauce**, *2 tbsp* **rice vinegar**, *3 tbsp* **sugar**, *1 tsp* **potato starch or cornstarch** dissolved in the liquid. Set near the stove.
- [ ] **Soup**: into a medium pot add *4 cups* water or light broth, *3 thin slices* **fresh ginger**, a pinch of **salt**. Bring to a gentle simmer. Hold on low heat.
- [ ] At **5:30** start **white rice** in the rice cooker.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Carrot ribbon salad**: into a serving bowl with the **ribbons**, scatter the **scallion greens**, drizzle generously with **soy-ginger dressing** from the fridge. Toss gently. Taste -- savory and clean. Refrigerate.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

**Wok dish 1 -- Orange chicken**: toss the **chicken chunks** thoroughly in *potato starch or cornstarch* until no longer sticky. Wok ripping hot, generous **neutral oil** -- enough to shallow-fry. Fry in batches without crowding, *don't move* until a firm crust forms, 90-120 seconds. Flip, 90 more seconds. The chicken is already cooked -- you're building crust only. Remove all to a plate. Pour off most of the oil, leaving a thin film. Add *2 cloves* **minced garlic** and *1 inch* **grated ginger**, stir 15 seconds. Add **scallion whites**, toss 15 seconds. Add **bell pepper strips**, toss 60 seconds. Pour in the **orange sauce** -- it will bubble and thicken fast. Return **chicken**, toss to coat. Off heat: ***taste*** -- adjust soy or sugar. Scatter **scallion greens** and **orange zest** over the top. Out.

**Wok dish 2 -- Broccoli**: quick rinse of the wok. Back to ripping hot, splash of **oil**. Dump in the **broccoli** from the bag, spread flat, *don't touch* 60-90 seconds until charred in spots. Toss. Add *2 sliced garlic cloves*, toss 20 seconds. Off heat: drizzle of **sesame oil**, generous scatter of **sesame seeds**. Out.

**Soup**: add a few **scallion greens** to the simmering pot. ***Taste*** -- adjust salt. Ladle into individual soup bowls at the table.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Orange chicken** in a center bowl. **Sesame broccoli**, **carrot ribbon salad**, and **soup** alongside.

## Burrito Bowl Spread

##### Prep&Heat+
###### lentil pot on + bean salad + rice toast + queso + wok charred salsa

#### Anchor:
Tex-mex **lentil picadillo** -- brown lentils simmered with chipotle, cumin, fire roasted tomatoes, garlic, onion. Thick, deeply spiced, scoopable.

#### Fan:
Fresh **Mexican rice** -- white rice toasted in oil with garlic, onion, tomato, and cumin. **Tortilla chips** on the side.

#### Cai:
Homemade **queso** -- roux-based, shredded melty cheese, one chipotle, cumin. Warm in a bowl, doubles as the chip dip.

Black bean + corn salad -- drained black beans, corn, diced onion, cilantro, lime, cumin. Cold.

Charred salsa cruda -- Roma tomatoes wok-charred whole, rough chopped with garlic, pickled jalapeño, cilantro, lime. Served warm.

### Already Prepped

- (nothing -- sous chef runs everything tonight)

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] **Lentil picadillo**: in a heavy pot heat *1 tbsp* **oil** over medium. Add *1 diced* **yellow onion** + *4 minced* **garlic cloves** + *1-2* **chipotle peppers** (minced from the can) + *1 tsp* **cumin** + *1/2 tsp* **coriander**. Cook, stirring, 3-4 min until fragrant. Add *2 cups* rinsed **brown or green lentils** + *1 full can* **fire roasted tomatoes** + *5 cups* **water** + *1.5 tsp* **salt**. Bring to a boil, drop to a simmer, cover. Cook **30 min** until lentils are tender. Uncover and simmer a few more minutes if too loose -- it should be thick and spoonable. ***Taste*** -- adjust salt and chipotle. Hold on low heat.
- [ ] **Mexican rice** (start ~30 min before serving): in a medium saucepan heat *1 tbsp* **oil** over medium-high. Add *1 cup* **white rice**, stir constantly until grains turn opaque and smell nutty, 3-4 min. Add *1/2* **yellow onion** (finely diced) + *2 cloves* **minced garlic**, stir 1 min. Add *3/4 cup* **water** + *3/4 cup* **chicken or vegetable broth** (or just 1.5 cups water) + *1/4 cup* crushed fire roasted tomatoes (from the lentil can), *1 tsp* **cumin**, *1 tsp* **salt**. Bring to a boil, cover, drop to lowest heat, cook **18 min**. Off heat, lid on, steam 5 min. Fluff with a fork.
- [ ] Drain and rinse *1 can* **black beans**.
- [ ] Drain *1 cup* **corn** (frozen thawed or canned).
- [ ] Finely dice *1/4* **yellow onion**, rinse under cold water, drain.
- [ ] Chop a generous handful of **cilantro** -- half for the bean salad, half for the salsa and table.
- [ ] Cut *2* **limes** into wedges. Set aside.
- [ ] Set *4-5* **Roma tomatoes** near the stove for charring.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Black bean + corn salad**: into a bowl combine the **black beans**, **corn**, **rinsed diced onion**, *half the cilantro*, juice of *1 lime*, *2 tbsp* **olive oil**, *1/2 tsp* **cumin**, generous **salt**. Toss. Taste -- savory, bright, clean. Refrigerate.

Open the **tortilla chips** and set on the table in the bag or a bowl.

### Chef - Cooking

**Queso**: in a small saucepan over medium heat, melt *2 tbsp* **butter**. Whisk in *2 tbsp* **flour**, cook 1 min. Gradually whisk in *1.5 cups* **whole milk**, whisking constantly until smooth and beginning to thicken. Reduce heat to low. Add *1.5 cups* **shredded melty cheese** (Mexican blend or American) in two batches, stirring until fully melted. Stir in *1 minced* **chipotle pepper** + *1/2 tsp* **cumin** + pinch of **salt**. ***Taste*** -- adjust salt and chipotle. Hold on lowest heat, stir before serving. If it thickens too much: splash of milk.

**Charred salsa**: Wok ripping hot, thin film of **oil**. Add **Roma tomatoes** whole, *don't move* 2 min until deeply charred on the bottom face. Roll to the next side, repeat -- work all sides until charred and beginning to collapse, 8-10 min total. Transfer to a cutting board. Rough chop. Into a bowl: the chopped tomatoes + *2 minced garlic cloves* + *1-2 pickled jalapeños* (finely chopped) + *the remaining cilantro* + juice of *half a lime* + pinch of **salt**. Stir. ***Taste*** -- loud, smoky, punchy. Serve warm in a shared bowl.

**Lentil check**: give the lentil picadillo a final stir and taste. Adjust salt. Ladle into a wide serving bowl.

**Table**: **Mexican rice** in a shared bowl alongside the **tortilla chips**. **Lentil picadillo** in a center bowl. **Queso**, **black bean + corn salad**, and **charred salsa** in shared dishes. **Lime wedges** and remaining **cilantro** on the table. **Greek yogurt** as a crema on the side.
