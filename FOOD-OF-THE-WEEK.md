## ✓ Weekend Prep

##### BatchDay
###### saturday: cube + bag pork into bath / sunday: chicken bag + veg bags

### Prep Checklist

**Saturday afternoon (right after grocery, before unpacking the rest):**

- [ ] Trim and cube **pork shoulder** into roughly *1.5-2 inch* chunks. Split evenly into two piles.
- [ ] **Korean bag** -- pork chunks + soy + sesame oil + minced garlic + grated ginger + sugar + black pepper + splash of rice vinegar. Vac seal flat.
- [ ] **Eastern Med bag** -- pork chunks + olive oil + cumin + coriander + smoked paprika + smashed garlic + bay leaves + black pepper + salt. Vac seal flat.
- [ ] Drop both bags in the bath at **158°F**. Leave running until Sunday afternoon (~18-24 h is the window, longer is fine).

**Sunday (after pulling pork, during the 2-hour chicken SV window):**

- [ ] Pull both **pork bags** from the bath, ice bath 30 min, refrigerate ***intact*** (don't break the vacuum seals -- those juices become night-of glaze).
- [ ] Bump bath to **183°F**.
- [ ] **Potato bag** -- small potatoes halved or left whole if tiny + drizzle neutral oil + pinch salt. Vac seal. SV 183°F / 30 min until just tender but still firm. Ice bath, refrigerate sealed.
- [ ] **Carrot bag** -- peeled carrot chunks + drizzle neutral oil + pinch salt. Vac seal. SV 183°F / 35 min until fully fork-tender. Ice bath, refrigerate sealed.
- [ ] Bump bath to **160°F**.
- [ ] **Chicken thigh bag** -- season with salt, black pepper, smashed garlic. Vac seal flat. SV 160°F / 2 h, ice bath, refrigerate.

### Workflow

Saturday is light. Cube the **pork shoulder**, mix two distinct flavor profiles into two bags, drop in the bath. The circulator runs unattended overnight.

Sunday: pull both **pork bags** first, ice them, refrigerate ***sealed***. Bump the bath to **183°F** and drop the **potato bag** (30 min) and **carrot bag** (35 min -- goes in first). While those run, prep the **chicken thigh bag**. When the veg bags are done, ice them and bump the bath to **160°F** for the **chicken thighs** (2 h). By the time chicken is done, everything is iced and labeled in the fridge: 3 protein bags + 2 veg bags. Active work is maybe 30 min spread across the morning.

### Protein Bags

**Korean pork** (Tuesday): cubed **pork shoulder** marinated in *1/3 cup* **soy sauce**, *2 tbsp* **sesame oil**, *4 cloves* minced **garlic**, *1 tbsp* grated **ginger**, *2 tbsp* **sugar**, *1 tsp* **black pepper**, splash of **rice vinegar**. Vac seal flat. SV 158°F / 18-24 h. Cool sealed, refrigerate. Scallion whites go in day-of, not the bag.

**Eastern Med pork** (Thursday): cubed **pork shoulder** rubbed with *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, *1 tbsp* **cumin**, *1 tbsp* **coriander**, *2 tsp* **smoked paprika**, *4 cloves* smashed **garlic**, *2* **bay leaves**, *1 tsp* **black pepper**, *1 tsp* **salt**. Vac seal flat. SV 158°F / 18-24 h. Cool sealed, refrigerate. Lemon zest goes on day-of, not the bag.

**Curry chicken thighs** (Monday): *2 lb* **boneless skinless thighs** seasoned light with **salt**, **black pepper**, and a few **smashed garlic cloves** (the curry carries most of the seasoning Monday night). Vac seal flat. SV 160°F / 2 h. Ice bath, refrigerate.

### Veg Bags

**Potatoes (Monday curry cai)**: small potatoes, halved or left whole if very small. Into a vac bag: drizzle of **neutral oil**, pinch of **salt**. Seal. SV at 183°F for 30 min until just tender but still firm. Ice bath immediately. Refrigerate sealed. Monday night: sous chef slices into matchsticks, chef woks.

**Carrots (Monday curry)**: peel and chunk *3-4* **carrots** into bite-size pieces. Into a vac bag: drizzle of **neutral oil**, pinch of **salt**. Seal. SV at 183°F for 35 min until fully fork-tender. Ice bath immediately. Refrigerate sealed. Monday night: open bag directly into the curry sauce with the bag juices.

## ✓ Japanese Chicken Curry

##### WokNightPrep+
###### prep + curry simmer + 5:30 rice

#### Anchor:
Japanese curry with sous-vide **chicken thigh** chunks, **carrots**, and **frozen peas** -- built off curry cubes loosened with stock. Mild, glossy, weeknight comfort.

#### Fan:
White rice

#### Cai:
Wok-fried potato strips with miso-yogurt crema and sliced scallion greens

Dressed greens -- romaine or bag mix tossed with the soy-ginger dressing from the fridge

### Already Prepped

- **Curry chicken thigh bag** in the fridge from Sunday SV (160°F / 2 h, fully cooked).
- **Par-cooked potato bag** in the fridge from Sunday SV (183°F / 30 min, firm-tender).
- **Par-cooked carrot bag** in the fridge from Sunday SV (183°F / 35 min, fully tender).

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Dice *1 large* **yellow onion** for the curry.
- [ ] Mince *3 cloves* of **garlic** and grate *a thumb* of **ginger** for the curry.
- [ ] Open the **chicken thigh bag**, drain juices into the curry pot, cube the thighs into bite-size pieces.
- [ ] Pull the **potato bag** from the fridge. Slice the par-cooked potatoes into thin matchsticks (*~1/4 inch*) for the wok cai. Set in a bowl near the stove.
- [ ] Slice *2* **scallions** -- whites set aside for the curry, greens reserved for the wok potato garnish.
- [ ] Tear or chop *half* of the **romaine head** (or half the bag mix) into a serving bowl, undressed. Cover, refrigerate. ***Save the other half*** for Wednesday's spaghetti night greens.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Curry**: Heat a heavy pot or Dutch oven over medium, splash of **neutral oil**. Sweat the **diced onion** with a pinch of **salt** until translucent and just starting to color, ~6-8 min. Add the **minced garlic** and **grated ginger**, stir 30 sec. Pour in *4 cups* of **water or stock** along with the chicken bag juices. Bring to a simmer. Open the **carrot bag** and add the carrots with their juices -- they're already fully cooked, just need 2-3 min to absorb the base. Off heat, break in the **Japanese curry cubes** (use package guidance for amount based on liquid). Stir until completely dissolved and the sauce thickens. Stir in the **cubed chicken** and a generous handful of **frozen peas**. Bring back to a gentle simmer for 2 min. ***Taste*** -- adjust with a splash of **soy sauce** for salt, *1 tsp* **sugar** for round sweetness. Hold on low.

**Miso-yogurt crema**: in a small bowl whisk *3 tbsp* **Greek yogurt** + *1 tbsp* **miso** + juice of *1/4 lemon* + *1 tsp* **sugar** + a tablespoon or two of water until pourable. ***Taste*** -- savory-creamy with a tang and a faint sweetness. Refrigerate until plating.

At **5:30** start the **white rice** in the rice cooker.

Right before sitting down, toss the **greens** with *2-3 tbsp* of the **soy-ginger dressing** from the fridge.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

**Wok dish -- Stir-fried potato strips**: The potatoes are already par-cooked -- the wok just builds crust. Pat the matchsticks dry with a towel. Wok ripping hot, *generous* **neutral oil**. Dump the potatoes in a single layer, *don't touch* for 60-90 seconds until the bottom faces are *deeply golden*. Toss, redistribute, stir-fry 2-3 more minutes until crispy-edged. Off heat, **salt** generously.

Pile onto a serving plate. Drizzle the **miso-yogurt crema** over the top in a zigzag. Scatter **scallion greens** over.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Curry** in a wide center pot or bowl with a ladle. **Stir-fried potatoes with crema** and **dressed greens** in shared dishes on the lazy susan.

## Korean Pork

##### WokNight
###### prep cai + 5:30 rice + wok pork

#### Anchor:
Bulgogi-leaning **pork shoulder**, sliced thin against the grain from the SV bag and wok-fired hot with the marinade juices reduced to a glossy glaze, finished with scallion whites and greens.

#### Fan:
White rice

#### Cai:
Cucumber kimchi salad -- smashed cucumber, kimchi, sesame oil, scallion (made day-of by sous chef)

Carrot-edamame salad -- soy-ginger dressed, topped with peanuts, scallion greens, and furikake at the table

### Already Prepped

- **Korean pork bag** in the fridge from Saturday SV (158°F / 18-24 h, fully cooked, fully marinated).

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Pour the **frozen shelled edamame** into a bowl of *cold* water to *thaw*
- [ ] Cut 5 **spring onions** into *seperate piles* of whites and greens
- [ ] Peel and *finely* mince a small thumb of **ginger**
- [ ] Peel and *ribbon* a few **carrots**
- [ ] Chop *half* a **cucumber** into *bite size* medium-small cubes
- [ ] *Roughly* chop **3 tbsp kimchi**
- [ ] *Roughly* chop a couple handfuls of **peanuts**

Set the **spring onion whites** near the stove for later.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Cucumber Kimchi Salad**: Into a bowl goes your **cucumber**, chopped **kimchi**, **1 tsp rice vinegar**, **2 tsp sugar**, the **ginger**, **the greens of your spring onions**, along with **2 quick dashes of fish sauce**, and an **assertive pinch of salt**. Toss together until coated, and cucumbers are slighty beaten up. *Taste and adjust*. Refrigerate to mingle.

**Carrot-Edamame Salad**: Into a bowl with your **ribboned carrots**, *thawed* and *thouroughly* drained **edamame**, *half* of your **chopped peanuts** (set the rest aside for topping later) and some **soy ginger dressing**. Toss to coat and taste for deliciousness.

At **5:30** start the **white rice** in the rice cooker.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

Pull the **Korean pork bag** from the fridge. Cut open over a bowl and drain the **marinade juices** into the bowl -- these are the glaze. Pull the pork chunks out, lay them on a board, and slice *thin against the grain* into ~1/4 inch slices.

**Wok dish -- Bulgogi pork**: Wok ripping hot, thin film of **neutral oil**. Lay the **sliced pork** flat in the wok, *don't move* for 60-90 seconds until the bottom faces are charred and the edges crisp. Toss, redistribute, char another 60 seconds. Pour in the **marinade juices**, let them bubble hard and reduce to a glossy syrup that coats the pork (~1-2 min). Add the **scallion whites**, toss 30 seconds.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Bulgogi pork** in a small platter or shallow bowl, center on the lazy susan. **Cucumber kimchi salad** and **edamame salad with toppings** in small shared dishes flanking it.

## Bacon Spaghetti

##### Prep&Heat+
###### prep + bean salad + 5:30 boil

#### Anchor:
Spaghetti tossed with a *fresca-leaning* tomato sauce -- **bacon** rendered into the fat, **garlic**, hand-crushed **canned whole tomatoes**, finished with black pepper and a squeeze of butter. Lighter than carbonara, simpler than amatriciana. The pot ***is*** the meal.

#### Fan:
Spaghetti (one-pot exception -- the pasta is the fan)

#### Cai:
Roman white bean salad -- **cannellini** with diced rinsed white onion, parsley, lemon, olive oil, generous black pepper. Cold, sharp, peppery.

Dressed greens -- **romaine** (the second half of the head from Monday) tossed with Italian-style dressing or olive oil + balsamic + salt + pepper.

### Already Prepped

- **Half-head of romaine** (or remaining bag mix) from Monday in the fridge, undressed.
- Half a bunch of **parsley** in the fridge, untouched (the other half is reserved for Thursday's Eastern Med pork).

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Drain and rinse *2 cans* of **cannellini beans**.
- [ ] Finely dice *1/2* of a **white onion**, then rinse the dice in a fine sieve under cold water for 10 seconds to mellow the bite. Drain well.
- [ ] Roughly chop *half* of the **parsley** bunch (save the rest for Thursday).
- [ ] Peel and slice *4-5* **garlic** cloves thin for the sauce, set near the stove.
- [ ] Chop the **bacon** (half pack from the fridge) into ~1/4 inch lardons, set near the stove.
- [ ] Open *1 can* of **canned whole tomatoes**, set near the stove with a bowl for hand-crushing.
- [ ] Tear or chop the **half head of romaine** into a serving bowl, undressed. Cover, refrigerate.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Roman white bean salad**: into a serving bowl combine the **cannellini**, **rinsed diced white onion**, **chopped parsley**, *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, juice of *half a lemon*, *generous* **black pepper** (this is the dish -- don't skimp), **salt** to taste. Stir gently. Let it sit at room temp for 15-20 min so the beans take on the dressing. ***Taste*** -- peppery, lemony, clearly olive-oil-forward.

At **5:30** fill the dutch oven with water, **salt it like the sea**, and bring to a boil. Hold at a low boil until the chef arrives.

### Chef - Cooking

In a wide deep skillet or another pot over medium heat, drop in the **bacon lardons**. Render slowly until the fat is fully released and the lardons are *just barely crisp*, ~6-8 min. Don't blast it -- you want fat, not blackened bacon.

Add the **sliced garlic** and a *small pinch* of **red pepper flake**, stir 30 seconds until fragrant but not browning.

Pour in the **canned whole tomatoes** with their juice, crushing each tomato by hand into the pan as it goes in. Season with a small pinch of **salt** (the bacon already brings salt). Bring to a simmer, ***lower*** to medium-low, let it break down 12-15 min until it goes from watery to glossy and the tomato pieces soften into the sauce.

Drop the **spaghetti** (most or all of the 2 lb bag) into the boiling water. Cook until *just shy* of al dente -- about a minute less than the package says. Reserve *1 cup* of pasta water before draining.

Drain the pasta and slide it directly into the simmering sauce. Add a generous splash of **pasta water** (~1/2 cup), toss vigorously over medium heat for ~1 min so the pasta finishes cooking IN the sauce and emulsifies with the bacon fat. Off heat, swirl in a **knob of butter** until glossy. Crack a *lot* more **black pepper** over.

Right before sitting down, dress the **greens** with Italian dressing or olive oil + balsamic + salt + pepper.

**Table**: pasta plated into individual wide bowls or pasta plates. **Roman white bean salad** in a small shared bowl with a serving spoon, **dressed greens** in a small shared bowl. Extra **black pepper** at the table.

## Eastern Med Pork

##### Prep&Heat
###### prep cai + 4:30 quinoa + cast iron sear

#### Anchor:
Cubed **pork shoulder** from the Eastern Med SV bag, ripping cast iron sear, finished off-heat with a warm aromatic shower of garlic, parsley, lemon zest, and rinsed diced yellow onion.

#### Fan:
Quinoa

#### Cai:
Spiced pumpkin spread with date-walnut garnish -- room temp, served on a shallow plate

Chickpea-cucumber salad -- cherry pepper relish, lemon, olive oil, with a dollop of Greek yogurt at the table

### Already Prepped

- **Eastern Med pork bag** in the fridge from Saturday SV (158°F / 18-24 h, fully cooked, fully spiced).
- The other half of the **parsley** bunch in the fridge.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] **Spiced pumpkin spread**: open *1 can* **pumpkin puree** (~15 oz). Stir in *2 tbsp* **olive oil**, *1 tsp* **cumin**, *1/2 tsp* **cinnamon**, *1/2 tsp* **allspice**, *1 clove* minced **garlic**, *2 tbsp* **lemon juice**, **salt** to taste until smooth. Spread onto a shallow plate or wide bowl, smooth the top.
- [ ] ***Date-walnut garnish***: roughly chop *6-8 dates* + small handful **walnuts** + drizzle **olive oil** + pinch **salt**. Put in a small jar next to the pumpkin plate. Family scatters at first portion so the texture stays distinct.
- [ ] **Chickpea-cucumber salad**: drain and rinse *1 can* **chickpeas**. Into a bowl: *2 tbsp* **cherry pepper relish**, *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, juice of *half a lemon*, *1/2* **white onion** finely diced and rinsed under cold water for 10 sec, **salt**, **black pepper**. Stir. Dice *1 cucumber* small and fold in. Transfer to a serving bowl, refrigerate. (Cucumber goes in today -- if it sat from Sunday it would be watery.)
- [ ] At **4:30**, rinse *1 cup* dry **quinoa** in a fine sieve under running water until the water runs clear (removes the bitter saponin coating). Add to the rice cooker with *2 cups* water and a pinch of **salt**. Set to cook, hold on warm.
- [ ] Finely dice *1/2* of a **yellow onion**, rinse under cold water for 10 seconds, drain ***thoroughly*** on paper towels. (Chef's off-heat shower -- must be dry to fold into hot pork.)
- [ ] Mince *3 cloves* of **garlic** for the chef.
- [ ] Chop the **parsley** -- save a small pinch to scatter over the chickpea salad before serving, rest goes to chef.
- [ ] **Zest** *1 lemon* with a microplane or fine grater for the chef. Cut the zested lemon into wedges for the table.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

Right before sitting down, sprinkle a small pinch of **chopped parsley** over the chickpea salad and place a small bowl of **Greek yogurt** on the table next to it.

### Chef - Cooking

Pull the **Eastern Med pork bag** from the fridge. Cut it open over a small bowl and drain the **bag juices** -- reserve these as a finishing splash. Lift the cubes out, lay them on a board, and pat each cube ***dry*** with a paper towel. Surface moisture is the enemy of the sear.

Get the **cast iron** ripping hot over high heat with a thin film of high-smoke-point **oil**.

Lay the **pork cubes** in a single layer with space between them, *don't move* for 60-90 seconds until the bottom face is *deeply browned and crusted*. Flip with tongs, sear another 60 seconds. Flip to a third face, 30 more seconds. The inside is already perfectly cooked -- you're just building crust.

Off heat: scatter the **minced garlic** over the cubes and toss for 20 seconds in residual heat. Add the **chopped parsley**, the **lemon zest**, and the **rinsed dried yellow onion**. Toss once or twice -- everything coated and warm, onion still crunchy, parsley still green.

Splash in *1-2 tbsp* of the reserved **bag juices** and a small drizzle of fresh **olive oil**. Final squeeze of **lemon** from a wedge over the top.

**Table**: **quinoa** in individual bowls. **Pork** in a small platter or shallow bowl, center on the lazy susan. **Pumpkin spread with date-walnut garnish jar** to the side, **chickpea-cucumber salad with yogurt** to the other side, **lemon wedges** scattered on the table.
