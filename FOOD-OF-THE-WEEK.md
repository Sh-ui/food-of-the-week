## Pumpkin Curry

##### ChefDish
###### 5:30 curry + hacked naan + salad

#### Protein:
Red lentils (meatless)

#### Description:
**Red lentils** and **canned pumpkin** simmered together in **coconut milk** with chunked **Yukon Gold potatoes**, **onion**, **ginger**, and **garlic**, spiced from the cabinet — **cumin**, **turmeric**, **coriander**, **cayenne** — until the lentils dissolve and the pot becomes thick, fragrant, and *deep orange-gold*. The **potatoes** absorb the spice while holding their shape. A piece of the no-knead dough pulled from the fridge, stretched thin, blistered in a *ripping hot* dry cast iron for a hacked **naan** alongside. Quick **romaine** salad dressed with **rice wine vinegar**, **sesame oil**, a few drops of **fish sauce**, and a pinch of **sugar**. Just the two of us.

### Already Prepped

 - No-knead dough was started Monday morning (flour, yeast, salt, water — mixed, covered, left on the counter to rise, then moved to the fridge). A small piece (~¼ of the batch) comes out for naan tonight. The rest stays cold for the pizza.

### Sous Chef - Prep

Dice 1 **onion**. Mince **garlic** (3-4 cloves) and **ginger** (a thumb-sized piece). Cut **potatoes** into 1-inch chunks.

In a medium pot over medium heat, sauté **onion** in oil until soft and starting to color. Add **garlic** and **ginger**, cook 1 minute. Add **cumin**, **turmeric**, **coriander**, and **cayenne** and let them bloom in the oil for 30 seconds until the kitchen smells alive.

Add **canned pumpkin**, **red lentils** (about ¾ cup for two), **potato** chunks, and **coconut milk**. Add a cup of water to loosen — the lentils will drink a lot of it. Season with salt and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook on low for ***20-25 minutes*** until **potatoes** are tender and **lentils** have dissolved into the sauce.

Pull the small piece of dough from the fridge. Stretch it thin on a lightly floured surface. Heat a cast iron skillet *ripping hot*, no oil. Cook the dough 2-3 minutes per side until it *blisters and chars* in spots. Stack and cover with a cloth to stay warm.

Tear **romaine** into a bowl. Whisk together a quick dressing with some olive oil, cardamom, and seasoned rice wine vinegar. Dress just before serving.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

***Taste*** the curry. More **fish sauce** for salt, **cayenne** for heat, **rice wine vinegar** if it needs a lift. The sauce should coat a spoon and be *fragrant and slightly thick*.

Serve over **white rice** or on its own in bowls. Hacked **naan** on the side. Dressed **romaine** alongside. A drizzle of **sesame oil** and a scatter of **sesame seeds** over the curry to finish.

## Braised Chicken

##### ChefDish+
###### dry brine prev night + 4:00 sear + braise

#### Protein:
Chicken legs and thighs (dark meat from whole chicken)

#### Description:
Skin-on **chicken legs and thighs** seared hard first to render fat and set color, then nestled skin-side up over a deeply aromatic **barley** bed — **barley** toasted in the same rendered fat, built into a braise with **ginger**, **garlic**, **scallions**, **miso**, **oyster sauce**, **fish sauce**, and shredded **cabbage** folded in. The whole pot braises covered in a moderate oven until the **barley** is swollen and tender and the liquid has been absorbed into the grain. Lid comes off and heat cranks up so the skin lacquers and goes *crispy*. Two people. Real dinner.

### Already Prepped

 - Whole chicken has been broken down: **dark meat** (legs, thighs) separated and dry-brined overnight with salt, white pepper, and a pinch of **sugar**. **White meat** reserved, vacuum-sealed, and refrigerated for the pizza later this week.

### Chef - Cooking

In a Dutch oven or heavy oven-safe pot, sear the **chicken legs and thighs** skin-side down in a slick of oil over medium-high heat — do not move them — until the skin is *deeply golden* and fat has rendered, about 6-8 minutes. Flip briefly to sear the other side. Remove and set aside.

In the same fat, cook diced **onion** (half), sliced **garlic** (3-4 cloves), and julienned **ginger** until soft and fragrant, about 2 minutes. Add the **barley** and toast it in the aromatics for 2 minutes, stirring so every grain picks up fat and color.

Add enough water to cover the **barley** by about an inch. Stir in a heaping spoonful of **miso**, a glug of **oyster sauce**, a splash of **fish sauce**, and a pinch of **sugar**. Taste — it should be savory, slightly sweet, fragrant. Fold in the shredded **cabbage** and the **frozen collard greens** straight from the bag.

Nestle the **chicken pieces** skin-side up on top so the skin sits above the liquid. Cover and transfer to a **325°F** oven. Braise for ***45-55 minutes*** until the **barley** is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

Remove the lid. Crank oven to **450°F** and return the pot for another ***10-12 minutes*** until the skin is *crispy and lacquered* and any remaining liquid has reduced around the **barley**.

Remove the chicken and rest on a board. Stir the **barley**, taste, and adjust — more **fish sauce** for salt, a splash of **rice wine vinegar** for brightness. Scatter **scallion** greens over the pot.

Serve **barley and cabbage** in wide bowls with the chicken pieces on top. A few drops of **sesame oil** over everything to finish.

### Leftovers

Pull any remaining dark meat off the bones. Next day: hot skillet, day-old **barley** or **rice**, the shredded chicken, cracked **egg**, **frozen peas**, **scallions**. Season with soy sauce, **sesame oil**, white pepper, **MSG**. Scatter **furikake** on top. Five minutes, completely different meal.

## Chicken Alfredo Pizza

##### SousVidePrep+
###### sous vide chicken morning + dough from fridge + 5:00 oven

#### Protein:
White meat chicken (breasts, reserved from whole chicken)

#### Description:
The no-knead dough stretched thick into a well-oiled sheet pan, Sicilian-style. The "sauce" is a quick **Alfredo** — **heavy cream** reduced with **garlic** until glossy, **Parmesan** stirred in. Topped with sous-vide **white meat chicken** pulled into pieces, sliced **onion**, small **broccoli** florets, a pinch of **cayenne** over the top, and a final heavy layer of **Parmesan**. Baked at *500°F* until the crust is *crispy and blistered on the bottom*, the cheese is *bubbly and spotted brown*, and the **broccoli** has *charred edges*. Finished with a scatter of fresh **parsley**. Feeds the whole family. The dough spent at least two days in the fridge for flavor development.

### Already Prepped

 - No-knead dough has been aging in the fridge since Monday — cold-fermented for 2+ days for better flavor and easier stretch.
 - **White meat chicken** (breasts, vacuum-sealed from the whole chicken breakdown) has been dropped into the sous vide bath earlier today (Chef sets temperature remotely from the app, 145°F). Remove from bag, pat dry, pull or chop into pieces.

### Chef - Prep & Assembly

Remove dough from fridge 1 hour before baking to relax.

Preheat oven to **500°F**, rack in the lower third. Coat a half-sheet pan generously with olive oil — more than seems reasonable, this is a Sicilian.

Slice 1 **onion** thinly. Cut **broccoli** into small florets — small enough to roast fully in oven heat. Set both aside.

In a small saucepan over medium-low heat, pour in **heavy cream** (about ¾ cup) with 2 smashed **garlic** cloves. Reduce until the cream coats a spoon and smells *nutty and rich*, about 8-10 minutes. Remove **garlic**. Stir in a generous handful of finely grated **Parmesan** until smooth and glossy. Taste — season with salt and a pinch of **cayenne**. Set aside warm.

Stretch and press the dough into the oiled pan all the way to the edges. Let it relax 10 minutes if it springs back; don't fight it.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

Spread the warm **Alfredo sauce** across the dough. Scatter the pulled **white meat chicken** evenly. Lay the sliced **onion** across the top. Distribute **broccoli** florets. Dust with a pinch more **cayenne**. Finish with a final heavy layer of grated **Parmesan**.

Bake for ***15-20 minutes*** until the bottom is *crispy and golden* (lift a corner to check), the top is *bubbling and spotted*, and the **broccoli** has *charred edges*. Rest 5 minutes before cutting.

Scatter fresh **parsley** over the whole pan before serving family-style.

## Lazy Italioafs

##### ChefDish++
###### roll prev day + oven roast + sauce reduction + mash

#### Protein:
Ground meat — cheapest available (pork, turkey, beef, or sausage)

#### Description:
Round **italioafs** — **ground meat** mixed with **panko**, **egg**, minced **parsley**, **garlic**, diced **onion**, salt, and white pepper, rolled tight and cold the day before — oven-roasted hard until the outsides are *deep brown* and a ***probe*** in the cold center reads **160°F**. While they roast, the **frozen marinara** (Ragu) reduces on the stovetop with **garlic** and a knob of **butter** into a glossy finishing sauce. Alongside: **Yukon Gold potatoes** mashed with **butter**, **heavy cream**, roasted **garlic**, and chopped **parsley** until *silky*. **Romaine** dressed in olive oil and **balsamic vinegar** on the table. **Cherry pepper relish** for heat. **Parmesan** grated over everything at the end. Pure comfort. Slides to Thursday, Friday, or Saturday.

### Already Prepped

 - **Italioafs** were rolled the day before and are sitting cold in the fridge. Cold meat holds shape, browns more evenly, and lets the ThermoWorks find the true cold center.

### Chef - Cooking

Preheat oven to **425°F**. Place the cold **italioafs** on a lightly oiled wire rack set over a sheet pan so heat circulates all the way around. Roast for ***25-35 minutes*** until the outsides are *deeply browned*. Use the ***probe*** to find the cold center — pull at exactly **160°F**. Rest 5 minutes.

While they roast, pour the **frozen marinara** into a wide saucepan over medium heat. Add a smashed **garlic** clove and let the sauce reduce and tighten, stirring occasionally, until it's *glossy and coating*. Finish with a knob of **butter** swirled off the heat. ***Taste*** — a pinch of **sugar** if too sharp, **MSG** if it needs depth.

Cover **potatoes** in cold salted water and bring to a boil. Cook until a knife slides through without resistance, about 20 minutes. Drain and steam-dry in the pot for a minute. Mash with **butter**, a splash of **heavy cream**, minced or roasted **garlic**, and chopped **parsley**. Season with salt and white pepper. Keep warm covered.

Dress **romaine** with olive oil and **balsamic vinegar**, a pinch of salt, just before serving.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

Spoon **garlic mash** onto each plate. Nestle the **italioafs** alongside or on top and spoon the reduced **ragu** over them. Scatter **parsley** and grate **Parmesan** generously over everything. **Romaine** salad and **cherry pepper relish** on the table.
