## Pork Pita

##### Prep&Heat
###### thaw pork + assemble + sweet potatoes

#### Protein:
Leftover frozen roast pork

#### Description:
**Leftover roast pork** thawed and warmed in a skillet until it picks up a little color, stuffed into **frozen pitas** with sliced **cucumber**, diced fresh **tomato**, thinly sliced **red onion**, and a loose dressing of **whole milk Greek yogurt** thinned with fresh **lemon juice** and a drizzle of olive oil. Fridge condiments on the table -- **pickled onion**, **olives**, whatever's open. Alongside: **sweet potatoes** halved and roasted *cut-side down* until caramelized, finished with a drizzle of **sesame oil** and a scatter of **furikake**.

### Already Prepped

 - Leftover roast pork is in the freezer -- move to fridge the night before to thaw, or quick-thaw in cold water the morning of.

### Sous Chef - Prep

Halve the **sweet potatoes** lengthwise. Rub the cut sides with oil, season with salt. Place cut-side down on a sheet pan. Roast at **425°F** for ***25-30 minutes*** until the cut side is *deeply caramelized* and a knife slides through without resistance.

Dice the **tomato**, slice the **red onion** thin. Slice the **cucumber** into coins or half-moons. Warm the **frozen pitas** directly on a rack in the oven for the last 5 minutes of the sweet potato roast, or in a dry skillet.

Whisk **Greek yogurt** with a squeeze of **lemon juice**, a pinch of salt, and a thin drizzle of olive oil until loose and spoonable.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

Warm the **pork** in a skillet over medium heat -- splash of water to loosen if needed, let it *pick up a little color*.

Drizzle the hot **sweet potatoes** with **sesame oil** and scatter **furikake** over the top.

Set everything out family-style -- **pitas**, **pork**, vegetables, **yogurt sauce**, and whatever fridge condiments want to come out tonight.

## Hambagu

##### ChefDish+
###### form prev night + broiler + probe + flavor cabinet glaze

#### Protein:
Ground beef

#### Description:
**Ground beef** stretched with **panko**, finely diced **onion**, **egg**, salt, and **white pepper** -- mixed just until combined and shaped into thick oval patties, *tighter than a burger, more tender than a meatball*. Formed the night before and kept cold so they hold shape and brown more evenly. Broiled under *ripping high heat* with a ***probe*** in the cold center until the outsides are *mahogany and caramelized*. Glazed in the final minutes with something built from the flavor cabinet -- **oyster sauce**, a touch of **mirin**, **sesame oil** -- lacquered and glossy. Served over **white rice** with a dressed **bag salad** on the side.

### Already Prepped

 - Mix and form patties the night before. Cold meat holds shape and browns better, and lets the probe find the true cold center. Cover and refrigerate overnight.

### Chef - Cooking

Set the broiler to *high*, rack positioned close to the element. Place the cold patties on a lightly oiled wire rack over a sheet pan.

Broil for ***8-12 minutes***, flipping once halfway, until the outsides are *mahogany and crisped at the edges*. Use the ***probe*** -- pull at exactly **160°F**. In the last 2 minutes, brush with the flavor cabinet glaze and let it *lacquer on*.

Rest 5 minutes.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

Serve the patties over **white rice** with the remaining glaze spooned over. Dressed **bag salad** alongside.

## Kimchi Chicken

##### ChefDish
###### blend kimchi + brush + roast high heat

#### Protein:
Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs

#### Description:
**Kimchi** blended with a splash of **sesame oil**, a little **fish sauce**, and a pinch of sugar into a *loose, rust-colored paste* -- brushed generously over **bone-in skin-on chicken thighs** and pressed into a coat of **sesame seeds**. Roasted at high heat until the skin is *lacquered, burnished, and fragrant*. Alongside: a quick slaw of **frozen chopped cabbage** and sliced **bell pepper** dressed with **rice wine vinegar** and **sesame oil**, plus warmed **edamame** tossed simply with **sesame oil** and a scatter of **furikake**.

### Already Prepped

 - Nothing required ahead -- this comes together quickly the evening of.

### Sous Chef - Prep

Thaw **edamame** in hot water or microwave. Toss with a drizzle of **sesame oil**, a pinch of salt, and a generous scatter of **furikake**. Set aside warm.

Thaw the **frozen chopped cabbage** in a colander, squeeze out excess water. Slice 1 **bell pepper** thin. Toss the cabbage and pepper with **rice wine vinegar**, **sesame oil**, and a pinch each of sugar and salt. Let it sit -- it improves as it rests.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

Blend a few generous spoonfuls of **kimchi** (including some brine) with a drizzle of **sesame oil**, a splash of **fish sauce**, and a pinch of sugar until a *loose, punchy paste* forms.

Pat the **chicken thighs** dry. Season with salt. Brush the kimchi paste over the skin side. Press firmly onto a plate of **sesame seeds** so they adhere. Arrange seed-side up on a wire rack over a sheet pan.

Roast at **425°F** for ***35-40 minutes*** until the skin is *deeply golden, lacquered, and the seeds are toasted* and the thickest part reads **165°F**. If the seeds start darkening too fast, tent loosely with foil.

Serve the thighs over or beside the **cabbage slaw** with the **edamame** on the side.

## Shrimp Pad Riff

##### ChefDish
###### 20 min active + noodles

#### Protein:
Shrimp

#### Description:
**Shrimp** cooked hard and fast in a *ripping hot* pan with **garlic**, sliced **bell pepper**, and **frozen collard greens** straight from the bag, then tossed with **udon noodles** in a quick sauce of **fish sauce**, sugar, **chili**, and **sesame oil**. Finished with crushed **roasted peanuts** and sliced **scallion** scattered over the top. Not quite pad thai, not quite stir fry -- something in between that's bold and fast without being heavy. No rice. The **udon** is the carb and that's enough.

### Already Prepped

 - Move **shrimp** from freezer to fridge the morning of to thaw, or quick-thaw in cold running water 20 minutes before cooking.

### Sous Chef - Prep

Cook **udon noodles** per package. Drain and rinse with cold water to stop cooking. Set aside.

Crush **peanuts** roughly -- a zip-lock bag and the flat of a heavy pan works fine. Slice **scallions**, keeping whites and greens separate. Slice 1 **bell pepper** thin.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

Get a wide skillet or wok *ripping hot*. Add oil. Cook the **shrimp** fast -- 60-90 seconds per side until *pink and just charred at the edges*. Remove and set aside.

In the same pan, add a touch more oil, toss in sliced **garlic** and scallion whites, then the **bell pepper** and **frozen collard greens** straight from the bag. Stir constantly on high heat for 2-3 minutes until the greens are wilted and the peppers have some color.

Whisk the sauce: **fish sauce**, a pinch of sugar, a splash of water, **chili** from the cabinet, **sesame oil**. ***Taste*** it -- it should land salty, sweet, spicy, and bright.

Add the **udon** and pour the sauce in. Toss everything together until the noodles are *coated and glossy*. Return the **shrimp** and toss once more.

Serve in wide bowls. Scatter crushed **peanuts** and scallion greens over the top.

## Spaghetti

##### Prep&Heat
###### brown sausage + simmer Ragu + pasta

#### Protein:
Italian sausage

#### Description:
**Italian sausage** removed from its casing and broken into chunks, browned in a wide pan until *caramelized and deeply fragrant*, then **frozen marinara** goes in and reduces around it into a *glossy, rich sauce*. A knob of **butter** swirled in off the heat. Spooned over **spaghetti** with **Parmesan** grated at the table and **cherry pepper relish** for anyone who wants heat. As simple as it sounds. The flex meal of the week -- if dinner doesn't happen, the browned sausage lives in the fridge all week as a lunch amplifier.

### Already Prepped

 - Move **frozen marinara** from freezer to fridge the night before to thaw.

### Sous Chef - Cooking

Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to boil. Cook **spaghetti** until *al dente*. Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.

In a wide pan over medium-high heat, brown the **Italian sausage** (casing removed) in a slick of oil, breaking it into chunks. Don't rush -- let it get *genuinely caramelized*. Drain off excess fat if needed.

Add the thawed **marinara**. Stir to combine. Simmer on medium-low for ***10-15 minutes*** until the sauce is *thickened and glossy*. Splash in pasta water as needed to loosen.

### Chef - Finishing & Plating

***Taste*** the sauce -- **MSG** if it needs depth, a pinch of sugar if it's sharp, a knob of **butter** swirled in off the heat.

Toss the **spaghetti** in the sauce. Serve in wide bowls with **Parmesan** grated over. **Cherry pepper relish** on the table.
