## Sunday Prep

##### BatchDay
###### 1.5-2 h ⏱, no stove, no sauces

### Prep Checklist

- [ ] Lemongrass pork bag -- mix raw ground pork with aromatics in the bag, seal, refrigerate
- [ ] Bulgogi beef bag -- slice sirloin thin, marinate in the bag, seal, freeze
- [ ] Spinach bag -- raw + neutral oil + salt, vac seal, refrigerate
- [ ] Green bean bag -- raw + oil + chili flakes + salt, vac seal, refrigerate
- [ ] Zucchini bag -- coins + olive oil + lemon zest + salt, vac seal, refrigerate
- [ ] Cauliflower bag -- florets + oil + turmeric + cumin + salt, vac seal, refrigerate
- [ ] Tadka base bag -- diced onion + garlic + ginger + jalapeño, vac seal, refrigerate
- [ ] Move pre-portioned beef bag from freezer to fridge **Monday evening** (thaw for Tue)

### Workflow

One **cutting board**, one **small tray** for the meats. Everything else lives in vac bags. No stove, no sauces, no cooking. Run protein bags first (raw meat on the tray), then wash the board and run veg bags.

### Protein Bags

**Lemongrass pork**: into a vac bag with **ground pork**: 2 stalks **lemongrass** (tough outer leaves stripped, tender core minced very fine), 4 cloves **garlic** minced, 1 small **shallot** minced (or 1/4 white onion), 2 tbsp **fish sauce**, 1 tbsp **brown sugar**, 1 tsp **black pepper**, pinch of **chili flakes**. Seal flat. Refrigerate -- use Monday.

**Bulgogi beef**: slice **sirloin** as thin as you can across the grain (a 30-min spell in the freezer makes this easier). Into a vac bag with: 4 tbsp **soy sauce**, 2 tbsp **brown sugar**, 1 tbsp **sesame oil**, 4 cloves **garlic** minced, 1 inch **ginger** grated, 1 tsp **black pepper**, splash of **mirin** or **rice wine**, pinch of **chili flakes**. Massage to coat. Seal flat. **Freeze**. Move to fridge Monday evening for Tuesday dinner.

### Veg Bags (raw, seasoned, ready for SV blanch)

**Spinach (Tue Korean cai)**: dump bag of **spinach** into a vac bag with a small drizzle of **neutral oil** and a pinch of salt. Seal gently (don't crush leaves -- low vacuum or just heat seal). Refrigerate.

**Green beans (Mon Vietnamese cai)**: trim **green beans**, into a vac bag with **neutral oil**, pinch of **salt**, pinch of **chili flakes**. Seal. Refrigerate.

**Zucchini (Wed Italian cai)**: cut **zucchini** into 1/2 inch coins, into a vac bag with **olive oil**, **zest of 1 lemon**, **salt**, several grinds of **black pepper**. Seal. Refrigerate.

**Cauliflower (Thu Indian cai)**: cut **cauliflower** into bite-size florets, into a vac bag with **neutral oil**, 1/2 tsp **turmeric**, 1 tsp **cumin** (ground), pinch of **salt**. Seal. Refrigerate.

**Tadka base (Thu)**: dice 1 medium **white onion**, mince 4 cloves **garlic**, mince 1 inch **ginger**, mince 1 **jalapeño**. Into a vac bag together. Seal. Refrigerate. This becomes the base for both the dal and the sabzi -- one bag, two uses.

## Lemongrass Pork

##### WokNight
###### wok sequence + SV green beans, 20 min ⏱

#### Anchor:
Caramelized lemongrass ground pork -- Vietnamese, fish sauce + lime + sugar lacquered

#### Fan:
White rice

#### Cai:
Wok-blistered green beans (from Sunday bag, SV-blanched, then wok-charred with garlic and lime)

Carrot ribbon salad with rice vinegar, chili, cilantro, sugar -- cold

#### Soup:
None -- the pork is intensely seasoned, two cai, full table.

### Already Prepped

- **Lemongrass pork bag** in fridge from Sunday.
- **Green bean bag** in fridge from Sunday.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

At ***5:00*** drop the **green bean bag** into the sous vide at **183°F**. Set a 15-minute timer.

Start a family-size portion of **rice** in the rice cooker.

Peel and ribbon **carrots** with a vegetable peeler -- aim for 2-3 generous handfuls of long ribbons. Into a bowl: juice of **1 lime**, **2 tsp rice vinegar**, **1 tsp sugar**, pinch of **salt**, half a thinly sliced **jalapeño**. Toss with the **carrots**. Refrigerate to crisp up.

Strip leaves from **cilantro** -- about half the bunch, gently chopped. Set aside in two small piles: one for the carrot salad finish, one for the pork at the table.

Slice **scallion** greens thin. Set aside.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

**Wok dish 1 -- Lemongrass pork**: heat the wok ripping hot, splash of **neutral oil**. Dump in the **pork mixture** from the bag. ***Don't touch it*** for 60 seconds -- let the bottom layer caramelize hard. Then break up and stir-fry, pressing the meat against the wok to build dark, sticky edges. About 5-7 minutes total. The fish sauce + sugar lacquer will turn deep mahogany. ***Taste*** -- if it needs more salt, add **fish sauce**. If it needs sweetness, a pinch of **sugar**. Finish with **scallion** greens and a generous squeeze of **lime**. Out into a serving bowl.

**Wok dish 2 -- Green beans**: pull the **green bean bag** from the bath, drain the liquid into the sink. Rinse the wok briefly. Wok ripping hot, splash of **oil**. Dump the **green beans** in, spread flat, *don't touch* until they blister and char in spots, about 60-90 seconds. Toss, add 3 cloves of sliced **garlic**, toss 20 seconds until fragrant. Out immediately. Squeeze **lime** over.

Toss the carrot salad with the reserved **cilantro** right before serving.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Lemongrass pork** in a center bowl with **cilantro** scattered over. **Green beans** and **carrot ribbon salad** in shared dishes on the lazy susan. Lime wedges on the table.

## Bulgogi Beef

##### WokNight
###### wok sequence + SV spinach, 20 min ⏱

#### Anchor:
Korean bulgogi -- thin-sliced sirloin, soy-sesame-sugar marinade, wok-charred fast

#### Fan:
White rice with furikake

#### Cai:
Spinach namul -- SV-blanched, dressed with sesame oil, soy, garlic, sesame seeds (warm or room temp)

Smashed cucumber kimchi salad -- cold, crunchy, sharp

#### Soup:
None -- the bulgogi sauce reduces into a glaze, two cai is enough.

### Already Prepped

- **Bulgogi beef bag** thawing in fridge since Monday evening.
- **Spinach bag** in fridge from Sunday.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

At ***5:00*** drop the **spinach bag** into the sous vide at **165°F**. Set a 5-minute timer. Pull and ice bath ***immediately*** when the timer rings -- spinach goes from perfect to sad fast.

Start **rice** in the rice cooker.

Smash **3 cucumbers** with the flat of a knife. Tear into rough bite-size pieces. Into a bowl: **3 tbsp kimchi** chopped roughly, **1 tsp sesame oil**, **1 tsp rice vinegar**, **1 tsp sugar**, pinch of **salt**, **1 minced garlic clove**, **1 tsp sesame seeds**. Toss with cucumbers. Refrigerate to mingle.

Once the spinach is ice-cold, squeeze it gently to remove water (don't murder it). Pile into a bowl. Dress: **2 tsp sesame oil**, **1 tsp soy sauce**, **1 minced garlic clove**, generous pinch of **sesame seeds**, pinch of **salt**. Toss. ***Taste*** -- it should be nutty, savory, garlicky.

Slice **scallion** greens thin for the bulgogi finish.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

**Wok dish 1 -- Bulgogi**: pull the **beef bag** from the fridge -- it should be fully thawed. Heat the wok until it smokes, splash of **neutral oil**. Dump the **beef and marinade** in. ***Spread it flat*** and don't touch it for 60 seconds -- you want char, not steam. Toss, sear the other side 60 seconds. Now stir-fry hard, pushing the beef against the wok edges where it's hottest. The marinade will reduce into a sticky lacquer in 3-4 minutes total. ***Taste*** -- if it needs salt, splash of **soy**. If it's flat, splash of **rice vinegar** or **mirin**. Finish with **scallion** greens. Out into a serving bowl.

The spinach namul and cucumber salad are already done from the sous chef phase -- no second wok dish needed tonight.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls, scatter **furikake** generously on top. **Bulgogi** in a center bowl with extra **scallion** scattered over. **Spinach namul** and **cucumber kimchi salad** in shared dishes. A small pile of **kimchi** straight from the jar can ride along on the table.

## Arrabbiata

##### ChefDish
###### one-pot pasta + SV zucchini, 30 min ⏱

#### Anchor:
Spicy arrabbiata with angel hair -- garlic, anchovy, chili, canned San Marzano-style tomatoes, parsley

#### Fan:
The pasta is the fan tonight (one-pot relax)

#### Cai:
Zucchini sauté -- SV-blanched, then wok-finished with garlic, lemon, parsley (warm)

White bean salad with cherry pepper relish -- cold, sharp, fridge garnish energy

#### Soup:
None -- pasta night, full table, two cai.

### Already Prepped

- **Zucchini bag** in fridge from Sunday.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

At ***5:00*** drop the **zucchini bag** into the sous vide at **183°F**. Set a 10-minute timer.

Drain and rinse the **canned cannellini** in a colander. Into a bowl: **2 tbsp cherry pepper relish**, **2 tbsp olive oil**, juice of **half a lemon**, half a thinly sliced **white onion** (rinsed under cold water to soften the bite), salt, pepper, half the chopped **parsley**. Toss with the **beans**. ***Taste*** -- it should be punchy and a little hot. Refrigerate to sit.

Strip and roughly chop the **parsley** (whole bunch). Half goes in the bean salad, half is for the pasta finish.

Mince 6 cloves of **garlic** and slice 2 more thin -- both go into the pasta. Set near the stove.

Open the **anchovy tin**, fish out 3 fillets, set on a small plate near the stove.

Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil for the **angel hair**.

### Chef - Cooking

**Sauce**: large skillet or wide pot over medium-low heat. Generous **olive oil** -- 4-5 tbsp. Add the **3 anchovies** and the **minced garlic**. Stir constantly with a wooden spoon -- the anchovies will dissolve into the oil in 1-2 minutes. ***Don't burn the garlic***. Add **1 tsp chili flakes** (or more if you want heat). Now pour in the **canned whole tomatoes** with their juice -- crush them with the back of the spoon as they hit the pan. Splash of **red wine** if you have any open. Salt lightly. Simmer **15-18 minutes** until the sauce thickens and the oil pools red on top. ***Taste*** -- it should be sharp, deep, slightly sweet from reduction. Adjust salt.

**Pasta**: when the sauce is 5 minutes from done, drop the **angel hair** into the boiling water. ***Time it carefully*** -- angel hair cooks in 3-4 minutes. Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining. Drop the drained pasta directly into the sauce, toss with tongs, splash in pasta water as needed for silkiness. Finish with the second handful of **parsley** and the **sliced garlic** raw -- it sharpens the dish.

**Zucchini**: pull the **zucchini bag** from the bath. In a hot skillet, splash of **olive oil**, dump the zucchini in. Toss for 90 seconds to char the edges. Squeeze of **lemon juice**, scatter of remaining **parsley**, salt to taste. Out.

**Table**: **pasta** plated in shallow bowls (or a single big platter to family-style). **Zucchini** and **white bean salad** in shared dishes. Extra **olive oil** and a wedge of **lemon** on the table. Skip parmesan -- the anchovy carries the salt.

## Red Lentil Dal

##### ChefDish
###### dal simmer + SV cauliflower + raita, 30 min ⏱

#### Anchor:
Tempered red lentil dal -- turmeric, ginger, tomato, finished with sizzling cumin-mustard tadka at the table

#### Fan:
White rice

#### Cai:
Cauliflower sabzi -- SV-blanched, wok-finished with onion, tomato, garam masala (warm)

Cucumber-mint raita -- yogurt, cucumber, mint, cumin, lime (cold)

#### Soup:
None -- the dal is brothy and substantial, two cai, full thali table.

### Already Prepped

- **Cauliflower bag** in fridge from Sunday.
- **Tadka base bag** (onion + garlic + ginger + jalapeño) in fridge from Sunday.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

Rinse **2 cups red lentils** in a fine mesh strainer until the water runs mostly clear. Into a heavy pot with **6 cups water**, **1 tsp turmeric**, **1 tsp salt**, and a **knob of butter** or **splash of oil**. Bring to a boil, ***skim any foam*** that rises, then reduce to a low simmer. Stir occasionally for the next 25 minutes -- they'll dissolve into a loose, creamy porridge.

At ***5:15*** drop the **cauliflower bag** into the sous vide at **183°F**. Set a 20-minute timer.

Start **rice** in the rice cooker.

Dice **1 cucumber** small. Strip and chop **mint** leaves -- about 2 tbsp. Into a bowl: **1.5 cups Greek yogurt** thinned with a few tbsp of water, the **cucumber**, **mint**, **1/2 tsp ground cumin**, juice of **half a lime**, pinch of **salt**. Stir. ***Taste*** -- it should be cool, herby, a little tart. Refrigerate.

Halve the **tadka base bag** -- about half goes into the dal pot when it's almost done, half stays for the cauliflower sabzi. Eyeball it.

Roughly chop **2 tomatoes** and set aside in two small piles -- one for the dal, one for the sabzi.

Strip the remaining **cilantro** leaves for the dal finish.

### Chef - Cooking

**Dal finish**: when the lentils are creamy (about 25 min in), stir in half the **tadka base** and one pile of **chopped tomato**. Simmer another 5-8 minutes until the onion softens into the dal. ***Taste*** -- it should taste rich and rounded. Adjust salt. If it's too thick, splash in water; if too loose, simmer down.

**Cauliflower sabzi**: pull the **cauliflower bag** from the bath. Heat a wok or large skillet, splash of **oil**. Add the remaining **tadka base**, stir-fry 2 minutes until fragrant and the onion turns translucent. Add the second pile of **chopped tomato**, **1 tsp garam masala**, **1/2 tsp ground coriander** if you have it, pinch of **salt**. Stir until the tomato breaks down, 2-3 minutes. Dump in the **cauliflower** with all its bag liquid. Toss to coat, let it sear briefly so the edges char. ***Taste*** -- adjust salt and garam masala. Out into a serving bowl.

**Tadka pour (the F-word move)**: small pan over medium-high heat with **3 tbsp ghee or butter**. Once it foams, add **1 tsp cumin seeds** (or **1 tsp ground cumin** if no whole seeds), pinch of **chili flakes**, and a **smashed garlic clove**. Sizzle 30 seconds until the seeds pop and smell nutty. ***At the table***, in front of everyone, pour this sizzling oil directly over the bowl of dal. The whole table will hear it.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Dal** in a wide shared bowl with the tadka poured at the moment of serving. **Cauliflower sabzi** and **raita** in shared dishes. Lime wedges on the table. Scatter **cilantro** over the dal.
