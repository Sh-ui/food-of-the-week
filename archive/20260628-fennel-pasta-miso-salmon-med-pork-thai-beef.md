## Weekend Prep

##### BatchDay
###### saturday: thaw pork shoulder + thaw/buy salmon / sunday: SV pork shoulder + pumpkin spread

### Prep Checklist

**Cooking for 2-3 this week** -- minimal spread per night: anchor + fan + 1 cai, no soup.

**Saturday:**

- [ ] Move **pork shoulder chunks** from the deep freezer to the fridge to thaw overnight.
- [ ] If salmon is from the freezer, move it to the fridge to thaw overnight. If buying fresh, Saturday is the day.

**Sunday:**

- [ ] **Med Pork pork bag** -- cube the pork shoulder into ~1-inch pieces. Toss with *2 tbsp* **olive oil**, *3 cloves* grated **garlic**, *1 tsp* **cumin**, *1/2 tsp* **cinnamon**, *1/2 tsp* **allspice**, *1 tsp* **salt**, zest of *1 lemon*. Vac seal flat. SV at **158°F for 4-8 hours** -- longer is fine, the fat renders and flavor deepens. Pull, cool on counter, refrigerate the bag.
- [ ] **Spiced pumpkin spread** -- open *1 can* **pumpkin puree**. Stir in *2 tbsp* **olive oil**, *1 tsp* **cumin**, *1/2 tsp* **cinnamon**, *1/2 tsp* **allspice**, *1 clove* minced **garlic**, *2 tbsp* **lemon juice**, **salt** to taste. Smooth and fragrant. Cover and refrigerate. Pull out 20 min before Med Pork night to serve room temp.
- [ ] **Date-walnut garnish** -- roughly chop *6-8* **dates** + small handful **walnuts** + drizzle **olive oil** + pinch **salt**. Jar and refrigerate alongside the pumpkin spread.

### Workflow

Saturday is two bag moves and a possible salmon buy. Sunday is one focused hour: pork shoulder cubed and into the SV bath, pumpkin spread stirred together in 5 minutes, date-walnut jar done. Everything cold and ready before noon. Weeknights are pull-and-finish.

### Protein Bags

### Cai Bags

## Fennel Pasta

##### WokNightPrep
###### poach brats + gremolata + bean salad + 5:30 noodle water

#### Anchor:
**Bratwurst** sliced into coins, poached first to parcook, then seared ripping hot in the wok with **fennel** and **onion** until the edges blister and caramelize. Gremolata -- parsley, lemon peel, garlic -- scattered at the finish.

#### Fan:
Garlic butter **egg noodles** -- cooked in the brat poaching water for extra depth, finished with a knob of **butter** and minced **garlic**.

#### Cai:
White bean salad with cherry pepper relish -- cold, punchy, olive oil, lemon. One shared bowl.

### Already Prepped

- Bratwurst ready from the fridge.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Prepare the **fennel** -- slice the pale stalks into thin coins, quarter the white bulb from root to tip and thin-slice each quarter. Reserve any bright green fronds for garnish.
- [ ] Slice *1* **yellow onion** into half-moons.
- [ ] Strip and roughly chop the **parsley** -- divide into two piles: one for the gremolata, one loose for the table.
- [ ] **Lemon peel**: use a vegetable peeler to get thin strips of yellow peel (no white pith). Set aside for gremolata. Cut the lemon into wedges.
- [ ] Mince *3-4 cloves* **garlic** -- 1-2 cloves for the gremolata, rest for the wok.
- [ ] **Gremolata**: finely chop the **parsley** and **lemon peels** together with *1-2 cloves* **garlic**. Set aside on a small board or in a jar.
- [ ] At **5:30**: get a large pot of water to a *gentle simmer*. Drop **bratwursts** in whole -- parcook at a low simmer, *not a boil*, ~10-12 min until cooked through. Remove and let rest 5 min. Keep the water on for the noodles.
- [ ] Cook **egg noodles** in the brat water to just shy of al dente. Drain, toss with a splash of **oil** to keep loose. Set aside.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

**Bean salad**: drain and rinse *1 can* **white beans**. Into a bowl: *2 tbsp* **cherry pepper relish**, *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, juice of *half a lemon*, **salt**, **black pepper**. Toss. ***Taste*** -- punchy and bright. Refrigerate.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

Slice the rested **bratwursts** into coins, ~1/2 inch thick.

Wok *ripping hot*, thin film of **neutral oil**. If using **anchovy paste**, add a pea-sized squeeze and let it melt into the oil -- 15 seconds. Add **brat coins** in a single layer -- *don't move* 60-90 seconds until the bottom face blisters and colors. Toss once to brown another face, ~30 seconds. Remove to a plate.

Into the same wok: **fennel** and **onion**. Stir-fry 3-4 min until just tender and caramelized at the edges. Return the **brats**, toss everything together.

Off heat: add the **egg noodles**, toss with a knob of **butter** and the remaining minced **garlic**. Residual wok heat finishes the noodles. Squeeze of **lemon**. ***Taste*** -- adjust salt.

**Table**: **noodles** portioned into individual bowls, **brat and fennel** distributed over the top. Scatter **gremolata** over everything. **Bean salad** in a shared dish on the lazy susan. Extra **lemon wedges** and loose **parsley** on the table.

## Miso Salmon

##### Prep&Heat
###### miso glaze + sheet pan in + 5:30 rice

#### Anchor:
**Salmon** fillets brushed with a miso-soy-sesame glaze, roasted at high heat with **frozen edamame** and **onion wedges** on one sheet pan. The edamame crisps at the edges; the glaze caramelizes to deep amber.

#### Fan:
White rice -- rice cooker at 5:30.

#### Cai:
Dressed greens -- salad mix tossed with soy-ginger dressing from the fridge. Light and cold against the rich glaze.

### Already Prepped

- **Salmon** thawed in the fridge from Saturday.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Line a large **sheet pan** with foil. Preheat **oven to 425°F**.
- [ ] **Miso glaze**: whisk together in a small bowl -- *2 tbsp* **white miso**, *1 tbsp* **sesame oil**, *1 tbsp* **soy sauce** (or dark soy), *1 tsp* **sugar**, *1 tbsp* **cooking wine** (sake, mirin, or the cabinet cooking wine), *1 tsp* grated **fresh ginger**. Set near the sheet pan.
- [ ] Cut *1* **yellow onion** into thick wedges (6-8). Toss with a drizzle of **oil** + pinch **salt**.
- [ ] Measure *1.5-2 cups* **frozen edamame** -- no need to thaw, goes on the pan frozen.
- [ ] Pat **salmon** fillets *completely dry* with paper towel.
- [ ] At **5:30** start **white rice** in the rice cooker.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

Arrange **onion wedges** and **edamame** on the sheet pan. Nestle **salmon** fillets among them skin-side down. Brush **miso glaze** generously over the salmon tops and lightly over the edamame and onion.

### Chef - Cooking

Slide the pan into the **425°F oven**. Roast **12-15 min** until the salmon flakes easily at the thickest point and the glaze has caramelized to deep amber at the edges. Edamame should have a few charred spots. No flipping needed.

Transfer to a serving platter, pile the **edamame and onion** around the salmon. Drizzle any pan juices over the top. Scatter **sesame seeds** and **furikake** over everything.

Toss the **greens** in a bowl with a few spoonfuls of **soy-ginger dressing** from the fridge just before serving.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Salmon platter** center on the lazy susan. **Dressed greens** alongside. Clean and fast.

## Med Pork

##### Prep&Heat
###### pull cai + 4:30 quinoa + cast iron sear

#### Anchor:
Cubed **pork shoulder** from the Med Pork SV bag, ripping cast iron sear, finished off-heat with a warm aromatic shower of minced **garlic**, chopped **parsley**, **lemon zest**, and rinsed diced **yellow onion**.

#### Fan:
Quinoa -- rinse well, rice cooker at 4:30.

#### Cai:
Spiced pumpkin spread with date-walnut garnish -- room temp, on a shallow plate.

White bean salad with cherry pepper relish + yogurt dollop -- cold, sharp.

### Already Prepped

- **Med Pork pork bag** in the fridge from Sunday.
- **Spiced pumpkin spread** in the fridge from Sunday.
- **Date-walnut jar** in the fridge from Sunday.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Pull the **pumpkin spread** from the fridge 20 min before dinner to come to room temp. Smooth it onto a shallow plate. Set the **date-walnut jar** alongside -- family scatters at first portion so the texture stays distinct.
- [ ] **White bean salad**: drain and rinse *1 can* **white beans**. Into a bowl: *2 tbsp* **cherry pepper relish**, *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, juice of *half a lemon*, *1/4* **yellow onion** finely diced and rinsed under cold water 10 seconds, **salt**, **black pepper**. Toss. Transfer to a serving bowl, refrigerate. Put a small bowl of **Greek yogurt** on the table next to it.
- [ ] At **4:30**: rinse *1 cup* dry **quinoa** in a fine sieve under running water until the water runs clear (removes bitterness). Rice cooker with *2 cups* water + pinch **salt**. Cook, hold on warm.
- [ ] Finely dice *1/2* **yellow onion**, rinse under cold water 10 seconds, drain ***thoroughly*** on paper towels -- must be bone dry for the off-heat shower.
- [ ] Mince *3 cloves* **garlic**. Roughly chop the remaining **parsley**. Zest *1 lemon*, cut into wedges.

### Chef - Cooking

Cut open the **pork bag**, drain the **bag juices** into a small bowl -- reserve these. Lift out the pork cubes and pat each one ***completely dry*** with paper towels. Surface moisture is the enemy of the sear.

Cast iron over high heat with a thin film of high-smoke-point **oil**, *ripping hot*. Lay **pork cubes** in a single layer with space between them -- *don't move* 60-90 seconds until deeply browned and crusted. Flip with tongs, sear another 60 seconds. One more face, 30 seconds.

Off heat: scatter **minced garlic** over the cubes, toss 20 seconds in residual heat. Add **chopped parsley**, **lemon zest**, and the **rinsed dried onion**. Toss once or twice -- everything coated, onion still crunchy, parsley still green.

Splash in *1-2 tbsp* of the reserved **bag juices** + drizzle fresh **olive oil**. Final squeeze of **lemon** over the top.

**Table**: **quinoa** in individual bowls. **Pork** on a small platter center. **Pumpkin spread** (date-walnut jar alongside for scattering). **White bean salad** with yogurt to the other side. **Lemon wedges** on the table.

## Thai Beef

##### Prep&Heat
###### 5:30 rice + one wok

#### Anchor:
**Ground beef** seared hard and fast in the wok until deeply browned and crispy at the edges. **Onion**, **garlic**, and **fresh ginger** stir-fried right into the fat. Big handfuls of **kale or collard greens** wilted in. Seasoned with **soy sauce**, **fish sauce**, and **sugar** for balance. Finished with **scallions**.

#### Fan:
White rice -- rice cooker at 5:30.

#### Cai:
The greens live in the dish. One-bowl night for 2-3 people.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] At **5:30** start **white rice**.
- [ ] Dice *1/2* **yellow onion**. Mince *4-6 cloves* **garlic**. Grate or finely mince *2 tbsp* **fresh ginger**.
- [ ] Strip **kale or collard greens** from the stems, tear into large pieces. Wash and pat dry.
- [ ] Slice **scallions** on the bias -- whites and greens separated.
- [ ] Measure into a small bowl: *2 tbsp* **soy sauce** (dark soy works), *1 tbsp* **fish sauce**, *1 tsp* **sugar**. Ready to pour.

### Chef - Cooking

Wok over *high heat* with a drizzle of **neutral oil**. Add **ground beef** in a loose layer -- *don't touch* for 2 min, let it sear and crust on the bottom. Break it up, continue cooking until deeply browned throughout with some crispy edges. Drain excess fat if pooling.

Push beef to the side. Add **onion**, **garlic**, and **ginger** -- stir-fry 1-2 min until fragrant, then toss with the beef.

Add the **greens** in big handfuls directly into the hot fat. Toss and fold until wilted and dark green, ~2 min.

Pour in the **soy-fish sauce-sugar bowl**. Toss everything together. ***Taste*** -- savory, slightly sweet, good depth. Adjust with more fish sauce for salt or a pinch more sugar to balance.

Off heat: scatter **scallion** greens over the top.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Thai beef and greens** ladled over or in a shared center bowl. Scallion whites on the table for crunch.
