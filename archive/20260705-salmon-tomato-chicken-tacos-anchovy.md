## Salmon Salsa

##### Prep&Heat
###### broiler straight from frozen + 5:30 rice + no-cook salsa

#### Anchor:
**Salmon**, still frozen, coated in oil and blackening seasoning, broiled directly under the top element until the crust chars and the center just flakes. No thaw, no pan to clean.

#### Fan:
White rice -- rice cooker at 5:30.

#### Cai:
Black bean and fire-roasted corn salsa -- cold, no-cook, doubles as topping and side.

### Already Prepped

- **Salmon** stays in the freezer until go-time -- nothing to prep ahead.

### Sous Chef - Prep (optional -- Chef can do solo, ~10 min)

- [ ] Drain and rinse *1 can* **black beans**. Drain *1 can* **fire-roasted corn**.
- [ ] Roughly chop **cilantro**. Thinly slice **scallions**. Cut **limes** into wedges, reserve juice from one.
- [ ] **Salsa**: black beans + corn + chopped cilantro + sliced scallions + lime juice + drizzle **olive oil** + **salt**. Toss. ***Taste*** -- adjust lime and salt. Refrigerate.

### Chef - Cooking

Set the oven rack one notch below the top, **broiler on high**. Line a sheet pan with foil.

Rinse any ice glaze off the **salmon** hunk under cold water, pat dry. Rub with a thin coat of **oil**, then a heavy, even layer of the **blackening spice** mix -- paprika, cayenne, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, oregano, plus **salt**. Don't be shy, frozen fish can take a thick crust.

Broil **15-18 min** (longer than a thawed fillet -- it's cooking from frozen), watching the last few minutes closely since broilers vary. The crust should char dark, center should flake easily at the thickest point. If the top blackens before the center's done, drop the rack one level and finish there.

At **5:30**, start **white rice**.

**Table**: rice in individual bowls, salmon on top, salsa spooned over generously. Lime wedges on the table.

## Tomato Chicken

##### Prep&Heat
###### one pot + no fan to cook -- crusty bread instead

#### Anchor:
**Chicken thighs** seared skin-side down, then braised in **fire-roasted tomatoes** with **onion**, **garlic**, **bell pepper**, **carrots**, and **celery** until the skin crisps and the sauce reduces. Cook the full batch tonight -- reserve roughly half the meat plus extra sauce for Chicken Tacos.

#### Fan:
Crusty bread, sliced, for sopping. No pot, no cook, no extra dish.

#### Cai:
None needed -- carrots, celery, pepper, and onion all cook right into the anchor. One-pot night.

### Sous Chef - Prep (optional -- Chef can do solo, ~10 min)

- [ ] Pat **chicken thighs** completely dry, season both sides with **salt**, **pepper**, and **smoked paprika**.
- [ ] Slice *1* **onion** into half-moons. Slice **bell pepper** into strips. Mince *3-4 cloves* **garlic**. Peel and slice **carrots** into coins. Slice **celery**.
- [ ] Slice the **crusty bread**, set out in a basket.

### Chef - Cooking

Large skillet or dutch oven over medium-high, skin-side down **chicken thighs** in a thin film of oil -- *don't move* 5-6 min until the skin is deeply golden and crisp. Flip, sear another 2 min. Remove to a plate.

Pour off all but *1 tbsp* fat. Add **onion**, **bell pepper**, **carrots**, **celery**, **garlic** -- stir-fry 3-4 min until starting to soften. Pour in **fire-roasted tomatoes**, scrape up any browned bits, simmer 2 min.

Nestle **chicken** back in skin-side up, partially submerged. Cover, simmer **20-25 min** until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened. ***Taste*** the sauce -- adjust salt.

**Reserve**: pull out roughly half the chicken pieces plus ~1 cup of sauce, cool, and refrigerate for Chicken Tacos.

**Table**: chicken and sauce in a shallow serving dish, crusty bread on the side for sopping.

## Anchovy Noodles

##### Prep&Heat
###### one pot noodles + no-cook salad

#### Anchor + Fan:
Wide flat noodles tossed in a garlic-anchovy oil -- savory, umami, melts into the noodles instead of tasting fishy. Scallions folded in at the end.

#### Cai:
Mandarin cabbage-almond salad -- cold, crunchy, sesame dressing. Classic Chinese-takeout-style side, built entirely from pantry cans and the other half of this week's cabbage.

### Sous Chef - Prep (optional -- Chef can do solo, ~10 min)

- [ ] Mince *4-5 cloves* **garlic**.
- [ ] Thinly slice **scallions**, whites and greens separated.
- [ ] Shred the remaining **cabbage** (from Chicken Tacos). Drain **mandarin oranges**.
- [ ] **Salad dressing**: whisk *2 tbsp* **rice vinegar**, *1 tbsp* **sesame oil**, *1 tsp* **soy sauce**, *1 tsp* **sugar**.
- [ ] **Salad**: toss shredded cabbage + mandarin oranges + **sliced almonds** + dressing. ***Taste*** -- adjust vinegar/sugar. Refrigerate.

### Chef - Cooking

Boil **noodles** per package until just tender. Reserve *1/2 cup* pasta water, drain.

Meanwhile, small pan over medium-low: **olive oil**, *4-6 fillets* chopped **anchovies**, and **garlic**. Melt the anchovies into the oil, 2-3 min, stirring -- they should dissolve, not fry. Add scallion whites, 30 seconds.

Add drained **noodles** to the pan, toss with a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen. Off heat, fold in scallion greens and a drizzle of **chili crisp** if using. ***Taste*** -- should be savory and glossy, not fishy.

**Table**: noodles in a shared bowl or portioned individually. Cabbage-mandarin salad alongside in a separate bowl, cold.
