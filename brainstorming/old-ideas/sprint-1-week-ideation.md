**Rules of the road:**

## Night 1 -- Dapanji (no potato)

**Anchor:** chunked thigh braised with cumin, star anise, Sichuan pepper, dried chili, garlic, tomato, onion. Bag Sunday with all the dry spice + garlic + oil. Day-of: wok, add chopped tomato + onion, simmer.

**Fan:** rice.

**Cai 1 -- Cold dressed cabbage** (locked: **sesame**)

Blanch + cold-shock cabbage (frozen ok). Dress: black vinegar + lots of **sesame seed** (optional: dry-toast seeds 30 sec in a pan -- not a batch-cook project) + sesame oil + tiny pinch sugar + minced garlic. Optional: shredded carrot for color/crunch, scallion, MSG pinch.

*Sesame carries the aromatic; vinegar is the sharp backbone.*

**Cai 2 -- Garlic green beans** (locked)

Dry-fry style: blistered hot in wok with a little oil, very dark spots, lots of sliced garlic at the end, salt, a tiny shake of black vinegar or soy at the very end if it wants it. Day-of, 8 min. Buy a small bag.

## Night 2 -- Mushroom cream chicken (locked)

**Anchor:** thigh seared, removed, mushrooms cooked down hard in butter, deglazed with white wine or sherry/madeira if you have it, cream in, thyme or tarragon, salt. Chicken back in to coat.

**Fan:** rice. (Holds the cream sauce great. No carb conflict here.)

**Cai 1 -- Mint butter peas** (locked) -- frozen peas, butter, fresh mint chopped in at the end, salt. 5 min.

**Cai 2 -- SV honey-pepper carrots** (locked) -- carrots in bag with honey + salt + white pepper + a knob of butter. SV bath. Torch to caramelize at plating if wanted.

**Bag Sunday:** seasoned thigh (just salt + black pepper + thyme, light) + carrot SV bag (honey + salt + white pepper + butter pat). Two bags.

*This reads "bistro plate" but the spread is real.*

## Night 3 -- Fish night (French lane locked)

**Anchor (locked):** SV salmon, finish **brown butter + capers + lemon** spooned at the table. Rice soaks the sauce.

**Cai 2 -- SV asparagus** (locked direction)

Bag Sunday: spears + minced shallot + lemon zest + black pepper + olive oil. Calcium lactate + baking soda pinch per the additives guide (firm + bright green through Friday).

Day-of: pull from fridge, quick torch for caramelized tips if desired, or just serve delicate and warm. **Lemon juice goes on the salmon** (brown butter), **lemon zest goes in the asparagus bag**.

*Shallot-zest-pepper-oil is complete. Doesn't need more.*

**Cai 3 -- Third cai, French/Med, 3 ingredients, bougie:**

Web search surfaced three real options. Fennel scratched (too strong for family).

Green beans and haricots verts are too close to asparagus -- same thin-green-veg slot. Need something **starchy, substantial, different texture entirely**.

**Starchy cold cai, 3 ingredients, liangban prep or close to it:**

- **Beets -- lemon + olive oil** (or Dijon). Roast or SV whole Sunday, peel, slice cold. 3 ingredients literally. Earthy-sweet, deeply colored, totally different from anything else on the table. Very French bistro. Buy: one bunch beets (cheap). SV is ideal -- bag whole with nothing, 185°F 90 min, perfect.
- **Cold chickpeas -- lemon + olive oil + flaky salt** -- you have dried chickpeas on hand. Cook Sunday, cool, dress cold with lemon + good olive oil + salt (+ optional parsley if you have it from kofta). Creamy, substantial, acidic, French/Med. Zero buy.
- **Cold green lentils -- Dijon + shallot** -- you have green lentils on hand. Simmer until just tender, cool, dress: Dijon + shallot + white wine vinegar + olive oil. Classic French lentilles vinaigrette. 3 ingredients. Shares shallot with asparagus bag. Zero buy. Very rice-killer (earthy + mustardy + sharp). The most French thing on the table.
- **Edamame -- lemon + olive oil + flaky salt** -- you have frozen edamame. Shell, blanch, ice, dress cold with lemon + olive oil + salt. Clean, simple, starchy. 3 min. But edamame might feel too light and Japanese-coded for this night.

**Cai 3 -- Golden beets** (locked)

SV Sunday: whole golden beets in a bag, nothing else needed, 185°F ~90 min. Peel cold (the skin slides). Slice into wedges or coins day-of. Dress: lemon + olive oil + salt.

**The "something more" -- pick one finisher:**

- **Pistachios** -- roughly chopped or crushed, scattered over the top. Green against gold, rich crunch, turns it from "a bowl of beets" into a bistro cai. Classic pairing, lasts forever in the pantry, small buy. *This is the one.*
- **Pickled shallot or thin raw white onion** -- sharp, acidic, cuts the sweetness of beets. Could quick-pickle some shallot in the same step you do the red onion for kofta night (two birds). Uses the lemon from the asparagus bag too. Free overlap.
- **Parsley** -- bright, herby, free overlap from kofta buy. Scattered over top, gives freshness without adding richness. Simple.
- **Combo:** pistachio + parsley over lemon-oil beets. Still just 3 or 4 components but feels complete -- crunch + herb + sweet-tart beet + good oil.

**Strongest version:** golden beet wedges + lemon-oil + crushed pistachios + scattered parsley. The parsley comes free from kofta shopping. Pistachio is the one small buy that does the most work.

*Locked: golden beets + lemon + olive oil + pistachios (+ parsley if you want it).*

## Night 4 -- Kofta (locked)

**Anchor:** ground beef kofta -- cumin, coriander, paprika, parsley, garlic, onion, mint, salt. Pan-seared oblong patties. Bag Sunday: mix raw beef + spices + minced garlic + onion, seal flat, refreeze.

**Fan:** rice (butter + turmeric pinch for color).

**Cai 1 -- Yogurt cabbage slaw** (locked)

Frozen cabbage blanched + cold-shocked (or just thawed and drained). Dress cold: yogurt + lemon + garlic + mint + salt. Creamy, cool, herby. The yogurt connects to the kofta lane. Zero buy -- cabbage and yogurt on hand, mint coming anyway.

**Cai 2 -- Med smashed cucumber + chickpeas** (locked direction)

Chinese smashed cucumber technique (拍黄瓜), Mediterranean flavor. Whack cucumbers, salt and drain 10 min, then combine with cold cooked chickpeas. Dress: lemon + garlic + olive oil + pinch cumin (already out for kofta) + salt. Optional: parsley scattered over.

*Cucumber brings crunch and freshness. Chickpea brings substance and body. Together it's a real dish, not just a bowl of cucumbers.* Completely Levantine/Med, wants rice, shares every ingredient with the kofta itself. Cook chickpeas Sunday, cool in fridge.

**Buy list for kofta night:** cucumber (2), mint, lemon (bag shared with night 3), parsley (shared with night 3). Red onion optional -- quick-pickle as a condiment on the table if desired. Everything else on hand.

**Table:** kofta in the center, yogurt slaw cold, cucumber-chickpea cold, rice. Olives and cherry pepper relish from your fridge can sit on the table as no-prep extras.
