### TODAY -- Pork Pita + Roasted Sweet Potatoes

**What it is:** Leftover frozen pork (thawed, shredded or sliced) stuffed into frozen pitas with chopped cucumber, diced tomato (buy), thinly sliced red onion, and a loose Greek yogurt dressing with fresh lemon. Fridge condiments on the table -- pickled onion, olives, whatever's open. Roasted sweet potatoes alongside.

**Grocery need:** 1-2 tomatoes. That's it.

**Sweet potato note:** Roast them hard -- 425°F, halved, cut-side down in oil until they caramelize. Maybe finish with a little sesame oil + furikake drizzle since we have both. That's the F-word touch.

### Hamburg Steaks

**What it is:** Japanese-style hambagu -- ground beef stretched with panko, diced onion, egg, salt, white pepper. Shaped thick. Broiled under high heat with a probe until the center reads ~160°F. Glazed at the end (options: soy + mirin reduction, or teriyaki vibe, or just a splash of oyster sauce and sesame oil). Served over white rice with a dressed bag salad mix.

**Grocery need:** More panko (running low), 1 bag salad mix.

**Notes:**
- Stretch the beef -- don't make it all-beef, keep it juicy and tender by going ~30% panko/binder
- The broiler glaze is the F-word moment here -- what direction? Soy-mirin? Cherry pepper relish melted in? TBD, open to riff
- This is basically Japanese hambagu meets broiler dinner -- keeps it different from last week's italioafs

### Kimchi Sesame Glazed Chicken Thighs

**What it is:** Bone-in or boneless skin-on chicken thighs. Blended kimchi becomes the glaze -- whiz it with a little sesame oil, maybe a touch of fish sauce and sugar, until it's a loose paste. Brush on, press into sesame seeds. Roast or broil until the glaze is lacquered and seeds are toasted. Served with rice + some kind of slaw.

**Slaw question -- two options, pick one:**
A. Buy 1 bell pepper + use frozen bag of chopped cabbage. Toss with rice wine vinegar, sesame oil, pinch of sugar. Simple. Cheap.
B. Skip slaw entirely, serve with frozen edamame warmed and tossed with sesame oil + furikake. Even simpler.

**Grocery need:** Chicken thighs (bone-in skin-on, ~5-6 pieces). Optionally 1 bell pepper.

---

### Shrimp Pad Thai Riff (Udon)

**What it is:** Not quite pad thai, not quite stir fry -- somewhere in between. Shrimp cooked hot and fast with garlic, bell pepper, and frozen collard greens (or frozen cabbage), tossed with udon noodles in a sauce built from fish sauce + a little sugar + chili + sesame oil. Topped with crushed toasted peanuts and sliced scallion. No rice -- the udon IS the carb and it hits different.

**Why this works:** Uses the frozen greens, the bell pepper we're already buying, the fish sauce + sesame oil we have. Shrimp cooks in 3 minutes. Family-friendly but has personality.

**Grocery need:** Shrimp (frozen bag), udon noodles (fresh or frozen preferred, dry works too), peanuts (roasted unsalted or just roasted), scallions.

---

### Spaghetti & Meat

**What it is:** Simplest possible -- spaghetti, a quick bolognese-style meat sauce built on the frozen Ragu as a base, browned Italian sausage or a small amount of ground beef stirred in. Parmesan at the table. Done.

**Why it's the 5th / flex meal:** Dry pasta keeps forever, Ragu is already in the freezer, and if this meal doesn't happen as dinner, the protein bought for it (sausage links) can be used as a breakfast or lunch add-on all week. Nothing goes to waste.

**Grocery need:** Spaghetti (or whatever long pasta), Italian sausage links (2-3 links, ~1 lb -- remove from casing and brown). Skip buying more ground beef -- hamburg steaks already using that up.
