## PROBLEM 2: Meal 4 Rework (Meatless)

**Direction:** Indian vegetarian classic with soul food fusion twist

### Indian × Soul Food Fusion Brainstorm

**Core Indian Dishes to Build From:**
- **Dal** (lentil curry) - creamy, spiced, served over rice - CLASSIC vegetarian anchor
- **Saag/Palak** (greens curry) - spinach/kale cooked down with spices and cream/butter
- **Chana** (chickpea curry) - could sub white beans
- **Aloo Gobi** (potatoes + cauliflower) - hearty veg combo

**Soul Food Elements to Fuse:**
- Collard greens technique (long slow cook with smoky flavors)
- Corn as sweet element (soul food staple)
- Butter/cream richness (southern comfort)
- Smoked paprika (smoky depth)
- Rice as base (both cuisines use rice)

### Fusion Directions

**Option A: "Dal Palak" with White Beans (Kale & White Bean Curry)**
- Base: White beans treated like dal (creamy, spiced curry)
- Greens: Kale cooked down like saag (Indian greens technique)
- Fusion: Add corn for sweetness (soul food element)
- Spices: Garam masala OR curry powder + smoked paprika (soul food smoke)
- Technique: Indian tadka (tempering) with cumin seeds, garlic, maybe mustard seeds
- Creamy finish: Butter + yogurt or cream (both cuisines love richness)
- Serve: Over rice (common to both)
- PRO: Classic Indian structure, inherently vegetarian, soul food richness
- TEXTURE: Creamy beans + wilted greens + sweet corn pop

**Option B: Black-Eyed Peas Curry (Soul Food Bean + Indian Spices)**
- Base: Black-eyed peas (soul food classic) OR white beans with black-eyed pea vibe
- Technique: Indian curry method (onion-tomato base, spices bloomed)
- Greens: Kale added in (like dal palak or collards)
- Corn: Added for sweetness
- Spices: Curry powder, turmeric, cumin, smoked paprika
- Finish: Butter, cream, maybe coconut milk
- PRO: Bean-forward, very soul food, Indian spice profile
- Would need black-eyed peas instead of white beans

**Option C: "Saag Paneer" but with Potatoes (Kale Curry + Potatoes)**
- Base: Kale cooked down to creamy saag (Indian greens technique)
- Protein substitute: New potatoes (cubed, roasted or simmered in curry)
- Corn: Added for sweetness (soul food element)
- Spices: Indian (cumin, coriander, garam masala) + smoked paprika
- Creamy: Yogurt or cream finish, lots of butter
- Serve: Over rice
- PRO: Potatoes make it hearty, kale is the star, very Indian structure

**Option D: White Bean "Dal Makhani" (Slow-Cooked Creamy Curry)**
- Base: White beans slow-cooked to creamy (like dal makhani technique)
- Add-ins: Kale wilted in, corn for sweetness
- Spices: Garam masala, cumin, smoked paprika (fusion spice blend)
- Creamy: Heavy cream/butter finish (dal makhani style)
- Technique: Slow simmer to develop depth (soul food patience meets Indian technique)
- Serve: Over rice
- PRO: Rich, luxurious, inherently vegetarian Indian dish

### Spice Blend Questions
- Do we have garam masala or curry powder? (or should we build from individual spices: cumin, coriander, turmeric, chili?)
- How spicy? (soul food can be mild, Indian can range - what's the vibe?)
- Smoked paprika: key soul food element - how much vs traditional Indian spices?

### Technique Questions
- Tadka (Indian tempering with whole spices in hot oil/ghee): adds aroma, traditional Indian technique
- Slow simmer vs quick skillet: dal makhani style (rich, slow) vs quick dal tadka (bright, fast)?
- Greens: Cook kale down completely (saag style) or keep some texture (collards style)?

## DECIDED: Meal 4 - "Gumbo-Style" Black-Eyed Peas & Greens (Option E + L Hybrid)

**Concept:** Creole-spiced gumbo with roux base + coconut milk, black-eyed peas, greens (sub for okra), bacon as flavor foundation

### Core Structure:
- **Base:** Black-eyed peas (soul food classic bean)
- **Greens:** Collard greens or kale (substitute for okra, adds soul food greens element)
- **Technique:** Gumbo-style roux (flour + bacon fat, browned until deep)
- **Spices:** Creole blend (thyme, bay leaf, paprika, cayenne, maybe oregano) - NOT Indian spices
- **Creamy element:** Coconut milk (fusion twist, adds richness)
- **Bacon:** Render bacon first, use fat for roux + crispy bits as garnish/mixed in
- **Vegetables:** Onion, bell peppers (holy trinity base), tomatoes, greens, black-eyed peas
- **Serve:** Over rice

### Technique Flow:
1. Render bacon, reserve crispy bits
2. Use bacon fat + flour → make roux (brown it, gumbo-style)
3. Add holy trinity (onion, bell pepper, celery if available) + garlic
4. Add Creole spices, bloom them
5. Add tomatoes (or tomato sauce/crushed)
6. Add cooked black-eyed peas + cooking liquid (or water/stock)
7. Simmer to develop depth (gumbo patience)
8. Add greens, cook down (soul food long-cook technique)
9. Finish with coconut milk (creamy richness, fusion element)
10. Serve over rice with bacon bits on top

### Okra Alternatives (Texture Considerations):
- **Greens only:** Collards or kale cooked long = silky, no slime
- **Bell peppers:** Already in holy trinity, adds body
- **Corn:** Could add for sweetness/pop (soul food element)
- **Mushrooms:** If on hand, adds umami meatiness
- **Potatoes:** Could add new potatoes for heft (not traditional gumbo but works)

### Questions to Finalize:
- **Greens choice:** Collards (classic soul food, long-cook) or kale (on hand, quicker)?
- **Other vegetables:** Add corn? Mushrooms? Potatoes? Or keep simple (peas + greens + holy trinity)?
- **Roux darkness:** Light (blond), medium (peanut butter), or dark (chocolate)? Darker = more flavor
- **Tomatoes:** Fresh diced, crushed canned, or tomato sauce? (affects thickness)
- **Spice level:** Mild, medium, or hot? (Cayenne/red pepper controls heat)

### Ingredient List Needed:
- Black-eyed peas (dried, need to cook, OR canned)
- Bacon (small pack, for fat + bits)
- Collard greens OR kale
- Bell peppers (holy trinity - on hand)
- Onion (holy trinity - on hand)
- Celery (holy trinity - need to buy? or skip?)
- Garlic
- Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
- Flour (for roux)
- Coconut milk (canned)
- Creole spices (or build: thyme, bay leaf, paprika, cayenne, oregano, maybe allspice)
- Rice (on hand)
