### Prep Checklist

- [ ] Move **pork shoulder chunks** from deep freezer to fridge to thaw overnight.

**Sunday:**

- [ ] **Eastern Med pork bag**: cube the pork shoulder into ~1-inch pieces. Toss with *2 tbsp* **olive oil**, *3 cloves* grated **garlic**, *1 tsp* **cumin**, *1/2 tsp* **cinnamon**, *1/2 tsp* **allspice**, *1 tsp* **salt**, zest of *1 lemon*. Vac seal flat. Cover, refrigerate. Serve room temp -- pull it out 20 min before Eastern Med night.
- [ ] **Date-walnut garnish**: roughly chop *6-8* **dates** + small handful **walnuts** + drizzle **olive oil** + pinch **salt**. Jar it and refrigerate alongside the pumpkin spread.

## Sausage Fennel Pasta

##### WokNightPrep
###### poach brats + prep gremolata + bean salad + 5:30 noodle water

#### Anchor:
**Bratwurst** sliced into coins, poached first to parcook, then seared ripping hot in the wok with **fennel** and **onion** until the edges blister and caramelize. Gremolata -- parsley, lemon peel, garlic -- scattered at the finish.

#### Fan:
Garlic butter **egg noodles** -- cooked in the brat poaching water for extra depth, finished with **butter** and minced **garlic**.

#### Cai:
White bean salad with cherry pepper relish -- cold, punchy, olive oil, lemon. One bowl.

### Already Prepped

- Bratwurst ready from fridge.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Separate **fennel** -- slice the pale stalks into thin coins, quarter the white bulb from root to tip and thin-slice each quarter. Reserve any bright green fronds for garnish.
- [ ] Slice *1* **yellow onion** into half-moons.
- [ ] Strip and roughly chop the **parsley** -- divide into two piles: one for the gremolata, one for the table.
- [ ] **Lemon peel**: use a peeler to get thin strips of yellow peel (no white pith). Set aside for gremolata. Cut the lemon into wedges.
- [ ] Mince *3-4 cloves* **garlic** -- 1-2 for the gremolata, rest for the wok.
- [ ] **Gremolata**: finely chop the **parsley** and **lemon peels** together with *1-2 cloves* **garlic**. Small jar or board, set aside.
- [ ] **Bean salad**: drain and rinse *1 can* **white beans**. Into a bowl: *2 tbsp* **cherry pepper relish**, *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, juice of *half a lemon*, *salt*, *black pepper*. Toss. ***Taste*** -- punchy, bright. Refrigerate.
- [ ] At **5:30**: get a large pot of water up to a *gentle simmer*. Drop **bratwurst** in whole -- parcook at a low simmer, *not a boil*, about 10-12 min until cooked through. Remove, let rest 5 min. Keep the water on for the noodles.
- [ ] Cook **egg noodles** in the brat water to just shy of al dente. Drain, toss with a splash of **oil** to keep loose. Set aside.

### Chef - Wok Sequence

Slice the **bratwursts** into coins, ~1/2 inch thick.

Wok *ripping hot*, thin film of **neutral oil**. If you have **anchovy paste**, add a pea-sized squeeze and let it melt into the oil -- 15 seconds. Then add **brat coins** in a single layer. *Don't move* 60-90 seconds until the bottom face blisters and gets some color. Toss once to brown another face, ~30 seconds. Remove to a plate -- they're done, just building crust.

Into the same wok: **fennel** and **onion**. Stir-fry 3-4 min until just tender and caramelized at the edges. Return the **brats**, toss everything together.

Off heat: add the **egg noodles**, toss with a knob of **butter** and the remaining minced **garlic**. The residual wok heat finishes the noodles. Squeeze of **lemon**. ***Taste*** -- adjust salt.

**Table**: **noodles** portioned into individual bowls. **Brat and fennel** distributed over. Scatter **gremolata** over the top. **Bean salad** in a shared dish. Extra **lemon wedges** and **parsley** on the table.

## Eastern Med Pork

##### Prep&Heat
###### pull cai + 4:30 quinoa + cast iron sear

#### Anchor:
Cubed **pork shoulder** from the Eastern Med SV bag, ripping cast iron sear, finished off-heat with a warm aromatic shower of minced **garlic**, **parsley**, **lemon zest**, and rinsed diced **yellow onion**.

#### Fan:
Quinoa -- rinse well, rice cooker at 4:30.

#### Cai:
Spiced pumpkin spread with date-walnut garnish -- room temp, on a shallow plate.

White bean salad with cherry pepper relish + yogurt dollop -- cold, sharp, fridge-ready.

### Already Prepped

- **Eastern Med pork bag** in fridge from Sunday.
- **Spiced pumpkin spread** in fridge from Sunday.
- **Date-walnut jar** in fridge from Sunday.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Pull the **pumpkin spread** from the fridge 20 min before dinner -- it should be room temp. Smooth it onto a shallow plate. Put the **date-walnut jar** nearby -- family scatters at first portion so the texture stays distinct.
- [ ] **White bean salad**: drain and rinse *1 can* **white beans**. Into a bowl: *2 tbsp* **cherry pepper relish**, *3 tbsp* **olive oil**, juice of *half a lemon*, *half* a small **yellow onion** finely diced and rinsed under cold water, **salt**, **black pepper**. Toss. Transfer to a serving bowl, refrigerate. Put a small bowl of **Greek yogurt** next to it at the table.
- [ ] At **4:30**: rinse *1 cup* dry **quinoa** in a fine sieve under running water until clear (removes bitterness). Rice cooker with *2 cups* water + pinch **salt**. Cook, hold on warm.
- [ ] Finely dice *1/2* **yellow onion**, rinse under cold water 10 seconds, drain ***thoroughly*** on paper towels -- must be dry for the off-heat shower.
- [ ] Mince *3 cloves* **garlic**. Roughly chop **parsley**. Zest *1 lemon*, cut into wedges.

### Chef - Cooking

Cut open the **pork bag**, drain the **bag juices** into a small bowl. Pat each pork cube ***completely dry*** with paper towels -- surface moisture kills the sear.

Cast iron over high heat with a thin film of high-smoke-point **oil**, *ripping hot*. Lay **pork cubes** in a single layer with space between -- *don't move* 60-90 seconds until deeply browned and crusted. Flip, sear another 60 seconds. One more face, 30 seconds.

Off heat: scatter **minced garlic** over the cubes, toss 20 seconds in residual heat. Add **parsley**, **lemon zest**, and the **rinsed dried onion**. Toss once or twice -- everything coated, onion still crunchy, parsley still green.

Splash in *1-2 tbsp* reserved **bag juices** + drizzle fresh **olive oil**. Final squeeze of **lemon** over the top.

**Table**: **quinoa** in individual bowls. **Pork** on a small platter center. **Pumpkin spread** (date-walnut jar alongside). **White bean salad** with yogurt to the other side. **Lemon wedges** on the table.

## Sheet Pan Miso Salmon

##### Prep&Heat
###### glaze + sheet pan in + 5:30 rice

#### Anchor:
**Salmon** fillets brushed with a miso-soy-sesame glaze, roasted at high heat with **frozen edamame** and **onion wedges** on one sheet pan. The edamame crisps at the edges; the glaze caramelizes to deep amber.

#### Fan:
White rice -- rice cooker at 5:30.

#### Cai:
Dressed greens -- salad mix tossed with soy-ginger dressing from the fridge, at the table.

### Already Prepped

- **Salmon** thawed in fridge from Saturday.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] Line a large **sheet pan** with foil. Preheat **oven to 425°F**.
- [ ] **Miso glaze**: whisk together -- *2 tbsp* **white miso**, *1 tbsp* **sesame oil**, *1 tbsp* **soy sauce**, *1 tsp* **sugar**, *1 tbsp* **cooking wine** (mirin, sake, or the cooking wine from the cabinet). Set near the sheet pan.
- [ ] Cut *1* **yellow onion** into thick wedges (6-8). Toss with a drizzle of **oil** + pinch **salt**.
- [ ] Measure *1.5-2 cups* **frozen edamame** -- no need to thaw, goes on the pan frozen.
- [ ] Pat **salmon** fillets *completely dry* with paper towel.
- [ ] At **5:30** start **white rice** in the rice cooker.

### Sous Chef - Assembly

Arrange **onion wedges** and **edamame** on the sheet pan. Nestle **salmon** fillets among them skin-side down. Brush **miso glaze** generously over the salmon tops and lightly over the edamame and onion.

### Chef - Cooking

Slide the pan into the **425°F oven**. Roast **12-15 min** until the salmon flakes easily at the thickest point and the glaze has caramelized to deep amber at the edges. Edamame should have a few charred spots. No flipping.

Transfer to a serving platter, pile the **edamame and onion** around the salmon. Drizzle any pan juices over the top. Scatter **sesame seeds** and **furikake** if you want.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Salmon platter** center. **Dressed greens** at the side. Clean, fast, no extra cai needed for 2-3 people.

## Thai Beef Stir-Fry

##### Prep&Heat
###### 5:30 rice + one wok

#### Anchor:
**Ground beef** seared hard and fast in the wok until deeply browned and a little crispy at the edges, then aromatics -- **onion**, **garlic**, **fresh ginger** -- stir-fried right into the fat. Big handfuls of **kale or collards** wilted in. Seasoned with **soy sauce**, **fish sauce**, and a spoonful of **sugar** for balance. Finished with **scallions** or torn basil if on hand.

#### Fan:
White rice -- rice cooker at 5:30.

#### Cai:
The greens live in the dish. One-bowl night -- no separate cai needed for 2-3 people.

### Sous Chef - Prep

- [ ] At **5:30** start **white rice**.
- [ ] Dice *1/2* **yellow onion** (or red if you grabbed one). Mince *4-6 cloves* **garlic**. Grate or mince *2 tbsp* **fresh ginger**.
- [ ] Strip **kale or collard greens** from the stems, tear into large pieces. Wash and spin dry (or just shake off -- they'll wilt down to almost nothing).
- [ ] Slice **scallions** on the bias if using.
- [ ] Measure into a small bowl: *2 tbsp* **soy sauce** (or dark soy), *1 tbsp* **fish sauce**, *1 tsp* **sugar**. Ready to pour.

### Chef - Cooking

Wok over *high heat* with a drizzle of **neutral oil**. Add **ground beef** in a loose layer -- *don't touch* for 2 min, let it sear and develop a crust on the bottom. Break it up, continue cooking until deeply browned throughout with some crispy edges. Drain excess fat if pooling.

Push beef to the side. Add **onion**, **garlic**, and **ginger** -- stir-fry 1-2 min until fragrant, then toss with the beef.

Add the **greens** in big handfuls directly into the hot fat. They'll steam and wilt fast. Toss and fold until collapsed and dark green, ~2 min.

Pour in the **soy-fish sauce-sugar bowl**. Toss everything together. ***Taste*** -- savory, slightly sweet, good depth. Adjust with a bit more fish sauce for salt or sugar to balance.

Off heat: scatter **scallion** slices or torn basil over the top.

**Table**: **rice** in individual bowls. **Thai beef and greens** ladled over or in a shared center bowl. Clean, fast, one-pan night.
